# HopeStack — Execution Plan
**Highest lifetime value · slowest, trust-heavy B2B cycle · start early.** Part of [Stage 9](00-master-plan.md). Source funnel: [05-funnels-claude/hopestack-funnel.md](../05-funnels-claude/hopestack-funnel.md).

> The counseling-center CRM built by a pastor, not a vendor — **HIPAA-aligned, pursuing SOC 2**. The goal for 90 days is not "sign up for software," it's land **5–10 design partners**. That's a relationships problem, not a marketing problem. The waitlist is the top of the funnel; the pilot conversation is the close. ([confirmed decisions #2](../CONFIRMED-DECISIONS-claude.md); [audit §2](../01-intake-and-audit-claude.md))

## Personas
- **Care Director Carol (buyer + champion).** Runs a counseling center or church care ministry, supervises 3–25 counselors, lies awake about (1) compliance/breach risk and (2) her counselors drowning in paperwork. Not a software person — evaluates "will this protect my people and free my team." Burned by sales-CRMs bolted onto care work.
- **Pastor Paul (introducer + validator).** Holds budget/trust, forwards HopeStack to his counseling director, vouches for Joshua. Already in the ecosystem (may buy consulting / Simply Pray Church first).

---

## 1. The 3 highest-leverage plays

**Play 1 — Founder-led direct outreach to a handful of warm centers.** Non-negotiable for a high-ticket, low-volume, relationship sale. Joshua doesn't need 10,000 followers; he needs 5–10 partners. List the counseling centers and church care ministries in his + Pastor Paul's networks, send personal notes (not pitches), book teardown calls. One pastor introduction beats a month of content. *From the [Dan Martell SaaS tutorial](../03-youtube-tutorials-claude/how-to-start-a-saas-business-from-scratch.md) (validate with real design partners) + [hopestack funnel, channel #1](../05-funnels-claude/hopestack-funnel.md).*

**Play 2 — Teach compliance, don't sell it (the Data-Protection Checklist).** The #1 magnet meets Carol exactly where her anxiety lives without making a claim HopeStack can't back. It lets Joshua talk about HIPAA-aligned design, encryption, row-level security, and audit trails as *education* — far more credible than the same words on a sales page — and it pre-qualifies leads (someone who downloads it feels the pain HopeStack solves). *From [hopestack funnel, lead magnet #1](../05-funnels-claude/hopestack-funnel.md).*

**Play 3 — The Design-Partner Pilot offer (honest scarcity).** Invite a vetted few into a relationship: free/near-free during beta, direct founder access, real roadmap influence, locked-in founding-partner rate later. The scarcity is *real* (a solo founder can only onboard a few well), so it never has to be manufactured — a hard requirement in this niche. *From [hopestack funnel, core offer](../05-funnels-claude/hopestack-funnel.md); "pick ONE channel, separate funnel from channel" from the [SaaS Academy bowtie tutorial](../03-youtube-tutorials-claude/ultimate-guide-demand-generation-for-saas.md).*

## 3. The decided specifics

- **Lead magnet:** "The Counseling Center Data-Protection Checklist: 12 questions to ask before you trust any software with client information" (PDF + companion email course). Secondary: "From Sticky Notes to Stewardship" (free 30-min workflow teardown — gate behind list engagement; it *is* the start of the sale). Third: "AI Session Notes, Done Right."
- **Landing page:** single job = right person joins the waitlist (one email). Hero options: "Care software built by a pastor, not a vendor" / "Protect the people you're trying to help" / "Your counselors were called to care, not to chase paperwork." CTA: **"Join the founding-partner waitlist."** Blocks: problem named honestly → "built by a pastor" story → 4 capability cards (intake & scheduling · AI session notes · supervision/role dashboards · audit trail) → **precise compliance block** → "what founding partner means" (3 bullets) → Scripture-rooted close → repeat CTA.
- **Welcome sequence (5 emails):** (1) deliver checklist + why a pastor built this, (2) "the 2 a.m. fear every care director knows" (breach anxiety → how the design eases it), (3) "called to care, not chase paperwork" (workflow win), (4) "using AI in counseling without crossing a line" (ethics → AI guide), (5) "we have room for a few founding partners" (pilot offer + teardown call).
- **Channels (exactly two):** (1) founder-led direct outreach + warm network (primary engine); (2) SEO + AI-search deep articles on Wisdom Walk / hopestack.tech (compounding). Social = distribution amplifier (LinkedIn for operational/compliance, faith groups for stewardship), *not* a build-from-scratch channel. Paid deferred.
- **Brand:** Navy `#0f1c33` · slate `#44516a` · amber `#eab44f` · sky `#60a5fa`. Competent, calm, trustworthy; vocabulary: secure, audit, role-aware, intake, scheduling, notes. No playful startup language. ([brand system](../08-brand-kits-claude/00-brand-system-claude.md))

## 5. Compliance guardrails — THE HARD LINE (legal, not style)
- **No PHI, ever** — never use/display/reference a real client's information in any marketing, screenshot, demo, or post. Fictional examples only.
- **No client stories/testimonials without explicit, documented consent** — and **never** from people receiving counseling. Even a design partner's quote needs written sign-off and zero identifying details.
- **No health-outcome claims** — HopeStack is care-management software; it does not treat, heal, or reduce relapse.
- **Precise HIPAA language only:** "HIPAA-aligned" / "designed to support HIPAA compliance" / **"pursuing SOC 2"** (or "SOC 2 in progress" if accurate). **Never** "HIPAA-compliant/certified," "SOC 2 certified," "fully compliant," "we make you HIPAA-compliant." Frame security as *design choices* (encryption, row-level security, audit trail), not guarantees.
- **No absolute security promises** ("unhackable," "100% secure," "can never leak").
- **No fear-mongering/guilt, no fake scarcity, no vendor-speak, no named-competitor attacks** (critique the category, not a company).
