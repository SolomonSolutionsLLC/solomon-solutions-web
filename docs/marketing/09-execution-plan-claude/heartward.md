# Heartward — Execution Plan
**Lowest ARPU → needs volume → SEO/AI-search is the moat.** Part of [Stage 9](00-master-plan.md). Source funnel: [05-funnels-claude/heartward-funnel.md](../05-funnels-claude/heartward-funnel.md).

> **Confirmed: Heartward is BOTH** a consumer theological-Q&A app (heartwardchat.com, 7-day free trial, $5/$12/$25 mo) **and** the underlying library/CMS platform with semantic search — **conservative-evangelical / Reformed lean** as the trust moat. *This plan leads with the consumer app* (the visible front, cheapest to reach via content) and runs the **B2B platform as a parallel, founder-led track** to seminaries/publishers/large churches. ([confirmed decisions #1](../CONFIRMED-DECISIONS-claude.md))
>
> The moat, plainly: depth in the Reformed/Puritan corpus + **real, numbered, verifiable citations**. *"Generic AI guesses about your faith. Heartward shows its sources."*

## Personas
- **Devout Dana (primary, the buyer).** 28–55, Reformed/confessional, studies, busy. Converts trial→paid because trustworthy cited answers solve her exact 10pm problem. Refers inside tight high-trust communities (r/Reformed, small groups, Reformed X).
- **Seeker Sam (secondary, the wide top).** 22–40, deconstructing/curious, asks existential questions privately. High search volume, slow gentle conversion. Nearly free to reach via the same content.
- **(B2B track) seminaries · Bible colleges · denominations · Christian publishers · large churches** — own corpora, want them searchable/answerable. Founder-led sales, not self-serve.

---

## 1. The 3 highest-leverage plays

**Play 1 — Make the product and the marketing the same activity (cited answer-articles).** Heartward's moat *is* its answers; publishing genuinely excellent, cited answers to the exact questions Dana and Sam type into Google/ChatGPT turns the core competency directly into distribution. It's the only channel that scales to a low-ARPU product's volume at ~$0 marginal cost, and it compounds for years. *From [heartward funnel, channel #1](../05-funnels-claude/heartward-funnel.md); "research-first beats AI slop" from the [Isenberg/Dickerson masterclass](../03-youtube-tutorials-claude/ai-marketing-masterclass-beginner-to-expert.md).*

**Play 2 — Win the AI citation (GEO/AEO), not just the rank.** Google AI Overviews are eating clicks (Ahrefs measured ~58% fewer); the new game is being the *source the AI quotes*, and Heartward's named Reformed/Puritan sources make it uniquely citable. Tactics: answer in the first 100 words; definitive (not hedged) language; FAQPage/Article/Organization schema; H2s phrased as the user's literal question; **cite primary sources by name** (Owen, Watson, Westminster); refresh monthly; allow GPTBot/Google-Extended/PerplexityBot. *From [heartward funnel, AI-search section](../05-funnels-claude/heartward-funnel.md) + [ai-search-aeo-geo.md](../02-research-claude/ai-search-aeo-geo.md).*

**Play 3 — The lead magnet IS a free demo of the promise (depth + receipts).** "10 Hard Questions… Answered from Scripture and the Puritans" answers 5 Dana + 5 Sam questions in 150–250 words each *with numbered citations to the actual works in the library*. The citations are the conversion mechanism: "I want this for *my* questions" → start the trial. Publish each answer as a web page too (gated PDF + ungated web = AI-search bait). *From [heartward funnel, lead magnet #1](../05-funnels-claude/heartward-funnel.md); "sell a specific transformation" from the [Tanner Chidester tutorial](../03-youtube-tutorials-claude/only-way-to-get-rich-with-digital-products-2026.md).*

## 3. The decided specifics

- **Lead magnet:** "10 Hard Questions About the Faith — Answered from Scripture and the Puritans" (PDF + email mini-course; serves both personas). Secondary (Dana): "The Reformed Reader's Cheat Sheet" (200+ works sorted by what they help with). Third (trust play): "Is This AI Safe for My Faith?"
- **Landing page:** single job = start the 7-day free trial (no credit card). Hero options: "Ask hard questions about your faith. Get answers you can actually trust — with the sources to prove it." / "Generic AI guesses about Scripture. Heartward shows its sources." / "Finally — an AI that knows the difference between Owen and opinion." Blocks: name the pain → **the demo (real question → cited answer, citations highlighted)** → 3 moat cards (Reformed depth · source-cited never invented · private & pastoral) → who it's for (Dana / Sam columns) → maker's note (Joshua, photo) → pricing (Disciple $12 "Most popular," **donation tiers OFF**) → FAQ (also AI-search fuel) → final CTA.
- **Welcome sequence (5 emails):** (1) deliver + the one promise (never invented citations), (2) "why does AI keep making up Bible verses?", (3) show a hard question get a real sourced answer → trial invite, (4) "is this safe for my faith? an honest answer," (5) gentle no-pressure trial invitation + blessing.
- **Channels (exactly two):** (1) SEO + AI-search content on Wisdom Walk + Heartward blog/FAQ (primary); (2) email (relationship + conversion engine). Social = supporting (X/Twitter Reformed community + IG/Threads quote cards). Paid deferred.

Brand voice + moat + guardrails carry over unchanged. *From [heartward funnel, "If Heartward is a B2B platform"](../05-funnels-claude/heartward-funnel.md).*

## 5. Compliance guardrails (what NOT to say)
- **No guilt/fear-based selling**, no spiritual shaming, no "you're failing God without this."
- **No fake scarcity/urgency.**
- **No overclaiming the AI's authority** — Heartward is a *study companion*, NOT Scripture, the Holy Spirit, the local church, or a substitute for your own Bible reading. Say so plainly; never imply it's infallible or inspired.
- **No claiming answers are perfect** — it's in active development and AI can err; position citations as the user's tool to verify (honesty is the differentiator).
- **Don't mix the donation ask with the purchase decision** — keep "Support the Mission" tiers on a separate page (guilt-leverage risk).
- **No theological overreach beyond the tradition** — be clear it reflects the Reformed/Puritan tradition specifically; don't present contested in-house debates as settled.
- **Privacy is sacred and a promise you must keep** — only market "we never share/sell your conversations" if the product/policy actually honor it.
