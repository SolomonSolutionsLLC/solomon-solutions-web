# The 7-Day ACTS Prayer Guide

*A simple way to pray, one day at a time.*

---

Most of us don't quit praying because we stop believing. We quit because we run out of words. You sit down, start with "thank you," then remember a bill, a kid's appointment, the thing you forgot to send at work, and ten minutes later you're scrolling your phone. Scattered prayer is normal. It isn't a character flaw. It's what happens when a heart with a hundred concerns sits down with no path to walk. This guide gives you a path. It's an old one, used by Christians for generations, with a name you can remember: ACTS. Spend seven days on it — not to perform, but to learn the shape of a conversation you can keep having for the rest of your life.

---

## What Is ACTS?

ACTS is four movements that carry you through a prayer. You don't have to spend equal time on each. You don't have to do them in lockstep. They're a trellis, not a cage — something for your prayers to grow on.

- **A — Adoration.** You begin with God, not with your list. You praise Him for who He is.
- **C — Confession.** You tell the truth about your sin and receive His mercy.
- **T — Thanksgiving.** You thank Him for what He has actually done.
- **S — Supplication.** You ask. For yourself, for others, for the world.

There's a reason for the order. Adoration sets the size of things right: God is large, your problems are real but smaller than they felt a minute ago. Confession clears the air. Thanksgiving reminds you He's been faithful before. Only then do you ask, now praying to a God you've remembered rather than a vending machine you're hoping works.

This week, focus on one movement per day for the first four days, then practice putting them together. Keep a pen nearby. Writing slows your mind down enough to actually mean what you say.

A note before you start: there's no streak to protect here. If you miss a day, pick it up the next day. God is not keeping score, and neither are we.

---

## Day 1 — Adoration

**Focus: Praise God for who He is, not for what He gives.**

Adoration is the hardest one to start with, because we're trained to ask first. But praise is where prayer was always meant to begin. When you tell God He is holy, you're not informing Him of anything. You're reminding *yourself* who you're talking to. The more clearly you see Him, the steadier you become. Name one thing that is true about God and sit with it.

> "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord of hosts; the whole earth is full of his glory!" (Isaiah 6:3)

> "Great is the Lord, and greatly to be praised, and his greatness is unsearchable." (Psalm 145:3)

**Pray and write:**

- Name three things that are true about God's character (for example: holy, faithful, patient, just). Don't rush.
- Pick one of those and tell Him, in your own words, why it matters to you today.
- Read Psalm 145:3 aloud, slowly. What does "unsearchable" mean for the thing you're worried about?

---

## Day 2 — Confession

**Focus: Tell the truth about your sin and receive His mercy.**

Confession is not groveling. It's honesty in the presence of someone safe. We hide because we're afraid of being seen, but God already sees, and He sent His Son anyway. So you don't confess to earn forgiveness. You confess because you already have it in Christ, and naming sin out loud is how you stop carrying it. Be specific. Vague confession ("forgive all my sins") keeps sin comfortable. Specific confession brings it into the light where it dies.

> "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." (1 John 1:9)

> "Create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit within me." (Psalm 51:10)

**Pray and write:**

- Ask honestly: where did I fall short this week, in thought, word, or deed? Write down one or two specifics.
- Confess them plainly to God. Then read 1 John 1:9 over what you wrote.
- Is there anyone you need to make something right with? Name them, and one step you can take.

---

## Day 3 — Thanksgiving

**Focus: Thank Him for what He has actually done.**

Confession and thanksgiving belong next to each other. After you've remembered your sin and His mercy, gratitude comes easily. Thanksgiving is different from adoration: adoration praises God for who He *is*, thanksgiving thanks Him for what He has *done*. It trains your eyes to notice. An ungrateful heart sees only what's missing; a thankful one starts catching evidence of God's kindness everywhere — in coffee, in a friend, in lungs that keep working without being asked.

> "Oh give thanks to the Lord, for he is good, for his steadfast love endures forever!" (Psalm 107:1)

> "Give thanks in all circumstances; for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus for you." (1 Thessalonians 5:18)

**Pray and write:**

- List five specific things from the past week you're thankful for. Push past the obvious.
- Thank God for one hard thing — not because it felt good, but because of what He may be doing through it.
- Notice 1 Thessalonians 5:18 says "in" all circumstances, not "for." What's the difference, for you, right now?

---

## Day 4 — Supplication

**Focus: Bring your real requests, and theirs, to the Father.**

Now you ask. "Supplication" is just an old word for asking, and it covers two things: your own needs (petition) and the needs of others (intercession). After three days of praise, confession, and thanks, you're not asking as a stranger. You're asking a Father you've been getting to know. Ask plainly. Jesus told us to pray for daily bread, which means the ordinary stuff counts. And don't only pray for yourself — carry someone else's burden to God today.

> "Do not be anxious about anything, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God." (Philippians 4:6)

> "Therefore, confess your sins to one another and pray for one another, that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person has great power as it is working." (James 5:16)

**Pray and write:**

- Write down three things you need right now. Be specific. Then ask.
- Name three people and one request for each. Pray through them by name.
- Is there an anxiety you're carrying alone? Philippians 4:6 says to make it known to God. Do that, in writing.

---

## Day 5 — Putting It Together (Short)

**Focus: Walk all four movements in one sitting.**

Today you stop practicing the parts and play the whole song. Keep it short; ten minutes is plenty. Move through Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, and Supplication in order. You'll feel the rhythm start to carry you. Don't aim for impressive. Aim for honest.

> "Pray then like this: 'Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name. Your kingdom come, your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.'" (Matthew 6:9–10)

Notice that even the Lord's Prayer starts with God's name and God's kingdom before it gets to "give us this day our daily bread." Adoration first, then asking. ACTS is just that pattern, slowed down.

**Pray and write (one line each):**

- **Adoration:** One true thing about God.
- **Confession:** One thing to bring into the light.
- **Thanksgiving:** One gift from this week.
- **Supplication:** One request for you, one for someone else.

---

## Day 6 — Pray for Others

**Focus: Spend your prayer time mostly on other people.**

Some days the best thing you can do is get your eyes off yourself. Intercession is one of the great privileges of being a Christian: you get to stand before God on behalf of people who can't, or won't, or don't know to. Paul did it constantly. Today, run the ACTS pattern, but let Supplication take most of the time, and let it be almost entirely about others.

> "First of all, then, I urge that supplications, prayers, intercessions, and thanksgivings be made for all people." (1 Timothy 2:1)

> "I do not cease to give thanks for you, remembering you in my prayers." (Ephesians 1:16)

**Pray and write:**

- Briefly adore, confess, and give thanks (a line each), then slow down for the rest.
- List five people: someone hurting, someone you struggle to love, someone far from God, a leader, and one more God brings to mind.
- Pray one specific thing for each. Then ask God how you might be part of the answer for one of them.

---

## Day 7 — Make It Yours

**Focus: Take ACTS off the page and into your normal life.**

The goal of this week was never to finish a guide. It was to start a habit. So today, pray the full ACTS pattern once more, and then think about how it lives past today. Maybe it's five minutes with coffee. Maybe it's a walk. Maybe it's the moment before you open your laptop. Prayer doesn't have to be long to be real. It has to be regular. Jesus, who had every reason to feel too busy, kept stepping away to pray.

> "And rising very early in the morning, while it was still dark, he departed and went out to a desolate place, and there he prayed." (Mark 1:35)

> "Rejoice always, pray without ceasing, give thanks in all circumstances." (1 Thessalonians 5:16–18)

**Pray and write:**

- Pray through all four movements one more time, unhurried.
- When and where will you pray this week? Write down a specific time and place.
- What's one thing you learned about God, or about yourself, in these seven days?
- Thank God for meeting you here. Ask Him to keep the conversation going.
