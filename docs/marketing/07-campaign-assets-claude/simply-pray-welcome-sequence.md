## Email 2 — The heart of ACTS (Adoration) and a quick win

**Send:** Day 2.

**Subject:** Start with who God is, not what you need
**Subject (alt):** The hardest letter in ACTS (and the best)

**Preview text:** Two minutes of Adoration changes the whole prayer.

**Body:**

Friend,

Most of us start prayer at S. Supplication. The list. "God, help with this, fix that, provide here." There's nothing wrong with asking. Jesus tells us to ask (Matthew 7:7). But starting there shrinks God to a vending machine.

ACTS starts somewhere else. Adoration. You begin by telling God who He is.

This is the letter people skip, because it feels awkward. We don't have words. So here's the quick win: borrow them.

Open the Psalms. Read one verse out loud back to God as praise.

Try Psalm 145:3 — "Great is the Lord, and greatly to be praised, and his greatness is unsearchable."

That's it. You just adored God. You didn't manufacture a feeling. You agreed with what's true. Do that for two minutes before your list tomorrow and watch how it reorders everything underneath it. The problems don't shrink. God gets bigger, so they sit in proportion.

Adoration isn't flattery. God doesn't need it. We do. We become what we behold.

Your guide has Adoration prompts on the first page. Use one tomorrow morning.

[**Open your guide to the Adoration prompts**]

Praying you'd see Him more clearly,
Joshua

## Email 3 — Forgotten and unanswered prayer; a record builds faith

**Send:** Day 4.

**Subject:** You've forgotten how God has answered you
**Subject (alt):** The prayers you can't remember praying

**Preview text:** Why keeping a record of answered prayer strengthens faith.

**Body:**

Friend,

Here's something I've seen in myself and in everyone I've pastored.

We pray for something for months. God answers. And within a few weeks, we've forgotten we even asked. The relief washes in, life moves on, and the answer evaporates from memory like it was never in doubt.

Then a new trouble comes, and we panic as if God has never once shown up.

Israel did this constantly. God split a sea, and a few chapters later they're sure they'll die of thirst. So God kept telling them: remember. Build a stone pile. Teach your children. "Remember the wondrous works that he has done" (Psalm 105:5).

We forget because we don't write it down.

A simple record of answered prayer is quiet fuel for faith. Not a guilt ledger. Not a scoreboard. Just honest notes: what you asked, the date, and later, how God moved. Some prayers get a clear yes. Some get a no, or a wait, or an answer you didn't expect. Write those too. Over a year, you end up holding evidence of a God who hears.

For now, start with paper. One line: "Today I asked God for ______." Date it. Come back in a month.

I'm building something to make this easier. More on that tomorrow.

[**Use the prayer log page in your guide**]

Grace and peace,
Joshua
