## TL;DR

- Marketing feels overwhelming because everyone selling marketing (the "tech bros," GaryVee, ad sellers) tells you their channel is the one true answer; the antidote is to **do less and be specific** so you protect cash flow as a bootstrapped company.
- The core mental model is to **separate two things that get conflated: the FUNNEL and the CHANNEL.** The funnel is the infrastructure (like bowling gutter guards) that turns attention into revenue; the channel is how you get attention into the funnel.
- The funnel follows the **buyer's journey: Awareness → Consideration → Decision → (then) Onboarding/Activation → Expansion → Promotion** — and most founders make the mistake of only marketing to the 10–20% who are ready to buy right now, ignoring the 80–90% who aren't yet.
- There are **only four channels to acquire attention: Paid, SEO, Earned Media, and Cold.** The coach (Marcel) favors earned media as a starting point because it balances risk, speed, and longevity, and it generates backlinks that make SEO stronger later.
- The action plan: **pick ONE channel and double down**, build your funnel **from the middle out** (start with how you talk about your product/website, then expand to the weakest area), and **audit your existing funnel** by red/yellow/green scoring each piece.

### 8. The funnel = infrastructure (the bowling gutter-guard analogy) (7:37–8:39)

### 15. The four channels — Paid and SEO (16:21–18:24)

### 18. Putting it together — one channel, build the funnel "middle out" (21:29–22:31)
- **Summary:** The execution plan: **Step one — choose ONE channel** ("just like the scaling credo, one channel") and double down to generate and accelerate attention. **Step two — build out your funnel, generally from the middle out:** start with **how you talk about your product (your website / decision-stage messaging)**, then expand toward whichever part is weakest.
