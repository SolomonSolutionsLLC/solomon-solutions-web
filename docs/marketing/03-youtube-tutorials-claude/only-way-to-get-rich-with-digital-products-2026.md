> **Jargon note for Joshua:** A few terms the creator uses without defining —
> - **Digital product:** anything you sell that has no physical shipping (a course, an app subscription, a PDF guide, a template). Simply Pray, Heartward, and HopeStack are all digital products.
> - **Low ticket / high ticket:** cheap offers (a few dollars) vs. expensive offers (hundreds to thousands). "Ticket" just means price point.
> - **Lead magnet:** a free or cheap thing you give away to collect contact info (an email address) so you can follow up later.
> - **Tripwire:** a very cheap first purchase whose real job is to turn a browser into a buyer, not to make money.
> - **Funnel / positioning / offer:** how you frame and present what you sell. "Positioning" = the angle/promise; "offer" = the full package (what they get + price + guarantee).
> - **Niche down:** narrow who you serve to a very specific group.

## TL;DR

- Digital products are not dead; **weak, replaceable offers are dead.** People can now "smell low value instantly," so generic courses, $5 PDFs, Notion templates, and prompt packs are struggling (especially without an existing audience).
- In 2026, **information is basically free** (creators post everything; AI tools summarize anything), so selling pure information means competing with free. What sells is a **clear, fast, low-risk outcome (a transformation)**, not content.
- The creator's six-part fix: (1) productize the **outcome**, not content; (2) bake in **implementation** (coaching, accountability, community); (3) build **proof loops** so buyers get a visible win in week one or two; (4) use a **low-ticket entry + high-ticket core**; (5) **niche down** and go deeper; (6) post **content daily** because "distribution is the business."
- The creator (Tanner Chidester) frames his authority around his businesses producing $141M+ over eight years and doing ~$53,000/day from digital products in 2026 — used as social proof for the method.
- His closing exercise: take your product idea and answer four questions — *What is the exact outcome? How fast can they get a win? What support makes it actually happen? What proof will it create?*
