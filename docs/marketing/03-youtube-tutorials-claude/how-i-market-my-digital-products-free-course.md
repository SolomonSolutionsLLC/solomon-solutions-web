## TL;DR (3-5 bullets)
- Maria Wendt says she makes at least $500,000/month selling digital products (her first $600K month happened recently), and she splits that revenue across exactly three marketing channels: **Instagram (25%), Email (25%), and Paid Ads (50%)**. She started at $63 in her first year.
- **Instagram** runs on a simple funnel: a piece of content (reel, carousel, or story) pitches a **keyword**, the viewer comments that keyword, and **ManyChat** software automatically DMs them a link to the checkout/sales page. About 6% of commenters buy.
- **Email** has two jobs done on a strict schedule: a **nurture email daily at 7 AM PST** (vulnerable, value-driven, no pitch) to warm the audience, and a **pitch email every other day at 1 PM PST** (very short, just a link to a $1 course or landing page).
- **Ads** are her biggest and (she argues) most beginner-friendly channel: simple **$5/day** ads that reliably produce sales within 24 hours. She runs three kinds: **lead ads** (collect emails), **retargeting ads** (recover near-buyers with a coupon), and **sales ads** (scale what works organically).
- Her single biggest piece of advice: **master one platform at a time** before adding the next. She built the channels one by one over years, and she tells viewers to test constantly and pick whichever channel feels best to start.
