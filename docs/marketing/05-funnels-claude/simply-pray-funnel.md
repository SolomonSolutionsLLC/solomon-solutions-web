# Simply Pray — Funnel & Growth Plan

> A pastor-built iOS prayer app that turns "I don't know how to pray" into a daily rhythm using the historic ACTS method — and gives churches a branded way to pray together.

---

**How to read this doc (plain-English note for Joshua):** A "funnel" is just the path a stranger walks from *never heard of you* → *email subscriber* → *paying customer*. Right now Simply Pray is **pre-launch** (not yet on the App Store), so the funnel's only job for the next ~90 days is to **collect a waitlist + email list** so you launch to a warm crowd instead of an empty room. Every recommendation below respects your real limits: ~$1,000/mo, 2–5 hrs/week, organic-first, faith-niche trust. Jargon is defined inline the first time it shows up.

**Urgent fix first (do this in week 1):** simplypray.io currently has a "Download free" button pointing to a **dead App Store page**. That is the single most damaging thing on the site — a broken link on a faith product silently destroys trust and wastes every visitor you'll ever earn. **Replace that button with a waitlist email capture** ("Be first to pray with Simply Pray — get launch access"). A dead button converts at 0%. A waitlist form on a faith product can realistically capture a meaningful share of visitors (see Conversion metrics). This one change is worth more than any ad.

## Primary audience

**Pastor Paul — the lead pastor who buys the Church plan ($49/mo).** This is the real money, and it's a *pastor selling to pastors*, which is your unfair advantage ("built by a pastor, not a vendor").

- **Who he is:** Lead/senior pastor of a small-to-mid church (75–400 attendees). Wears every hat. Cares about whether his people actually pray during the week, not just on Sunday. Time-poor, skeptical of slick software vendors, moved by a peer who *gets* ministry.
- **What he wants:** His congregation praying together between Sundays; a tool he can put his church's name on; something that makes him look like a thoughtful shepherd, not a tech gimmick.
- **What he fears:** Wasting the church's money, looking like he's chasing trends, a tool nobody uses, handing member data to a faceless company.
- **Why he's primary:** One Church sale ($49/mo = ~$588/yr) is worth ~28 Personal subscriptions ($21.24/yr). And each Church account **seeds dozens of free congregational users** — your B2C funnel and B2B funnel feed each other. (B2B2C = business-to-business-to-consumer: you sell to the church, the church gives it to its members.)

## Secondary audience

**Devout Dana — the individual believer (B2C).** Already a Christian, wants to pray more consistently but feels scattered, guilty, or stuck ("I run out of things to say"). Low price point ($2.99–$6.99/mo), high volume, low effort to acquire through organic content. Dana is your **proof-of-message engine**: she's cheaper and faster to reach, her behavior tells you which messages work, and a happy Dana who says "my church should have this" becomes your warmest path to a Pastor Paul. Treat Dana as both a revenue line *and* free market research for the Church pitch.

## Core offer

**Pre-launch (now):** "Reserve your spot — be among the first to pray with Simply Pray." The offer is *early access + a founding-member feel*, not a download. Free to join the waitlist; you're trading your email for launch-day access and a lead magnet (below).

**At launch (staged, download-ready):**
- **Individuals:** "Never run out of words to pray again." Free tier to start; Personal $2.99/mo (or $21.24/yr — frame as *"about 6 cents a day"*); Community $6.99/mo for families/small groups.
- **Churches (the headline B2B offer):** "Give your whole church one place to pray — with your name on it." Church $49/mo = branded prayer page, admin dashboard, member management. Frame it as *"less than the cost of one Sunday's coffee, for your entire congregation."*

The ACTS method (Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication) is the **product's spine and its #1 marketing asset** — it's a 500-year-old, trusted, teachable framework. Lead with the *method*, not the app. People trust a method; they're suspicious of an app.

## Lead magnet ideas (3, ranked, with why each works)

A "lead magnet" = a free, genuinely useful thing you give away in exchange for an email address. It's the trade that grows your list.

**1. "The ACTS Prayer Guide" — a beautiful 1-page printable + 7-day email prayer journey. (TOP PICK)**
- *What it is:* A single elegant PDF (Playfair + DM Sans, forest/gold/cream) walking through Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication with example prayers and Scripture for each — plus a 7-day email mini-series that delivers one ACTS step per day with a short prompt.
- *Why it works:* It **is** the app's core value, delivered free, so the people who download it are pre-qualified to love the app. It's instantly useful (solves "I don't know how to pray" today, no app required), printable (pastors will hand it out — that's organic distribution into churches), and the 7-day email series doubles as the front of your nurture sequence. Faith audiences love a tangible, Scripture-rooted tool. Low effort: you can draft it in Canva in an afternoon.

**2. "Pray Together: A Pastor's Toolkit for a Praying Church" (the Pastor Paul magnet).**
- *What it is:* A short guide (PDF or Google Doc) on building a culture of weekly prayer in a congregation — a 4-week prayer-challenge template, a sample bulletin insert, and a "how to launch church-wide prayer" checklist. Soft mention of the Church plan at the end.
- *Why it works:* It speaks directly to your highest-value buyer's actual job, positions you as a peer who's solved this, and pre-sells the Church plan without selling. A pastor who downloads this is a qualified B2B lead. Pair it with a "Book a 15-min call" option for the warmest ones.

**3. "30 Prayers for When You Don't Have Words" — a free email-delivered devotional pack.**
- *What it is:* 30 short, Scripture-anchored written prayers (grief, anxiety, gratitude, a wandering child, a hard marriage…) delivered as a PDF or drip.
- *Why it works:* Hits the exact emotional moment Dana searches in — "prayer for when you don't know what to say." Highly shareable, very low production cost, strong SEO/social pull. Ranks #3 only because it's less tightly bound to the ACTS method than #1, so it qualifies leads slightly less precisely.

## Landing page angle (hero headline options + the page's single job + copy blocks)

**The page's single job:** Capture an email for the waitlist. One job. Not "explain everything" — *get the email.* Every element points at one form with one field (email) and one button. (Single-field forms convert meaningfully better than multi-field forms.)

**Hero headline options (A/B test these — "A/B test" = show two versions, keep the winner):**
- **A (method-led):** "Learn to pray — one honest step at a time." Sub: "Simply Pray guides you through the time-tested ACTS rhythm: Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication. Built by a pastor, not a vendor."
- **B (pain-led):** "Ever sit down to pray and run out of words?" Sub: "Simply Pray gives you a gentle, Scripture-rooted path through prayer — every single day."
- **C (pre-launch urgency):** "Be among the first to pray with Simply Pray." Sub: "We're launching soon. Reserve your spot and we'll send you the ACTS Prayer Guide today."

**Primary CTA (call-to-action = the button):** "Reserve my spot" or "Send me the free guide" (better than "Submit" — it names the reward).

**Copy blocks, top to bottom:**
1. **Hero** — headline + subhead + email field + button. Above the fold, nothing else competing.
2. **The problem, named gently** — "Prayer can feel like a blank page. You're not alone — and you're not doing it wrong." (No guilt. Faith niche = anti-manipulation.)
3. **The ACTS method, explained in 4 simple cards** — Adoration / Confession / Thanksgiving / Supplication, one line each. This teaches value before they ever download.
4. **Built by a pastor, not a vendor** — 2–3 sentences of Joshua's story. *This is your moat.* Trust is the whole game in this niche.
5. **For churches** — a distinct band: "Lead a praying church. Get a branded prayer page, an admin dashboard, and your whole congregation in one place — for $49/mo." CTA: "See the Church plan" → a short B2B section or a Calendly/booking link.
6. **What you'll get for joining now** — "The free ACTS Prayer Guide + early access + founding-member updates."
7. **Repeat email capture** + a one-line Scripture footer (e.g. Romans 8:26). Keep it warm, not loud.

Mobile-first is non-negotiable — the overwhelming majority of landing-page traffic is mobile ([SEO Sherpa, 2026](https://seosherpa.com/landing-page-statistics/)), and you lose ~7% of conversions per extra second of load time ([Waitlister, 2026](https://waitlister.me/growth-hub/guides/waitlist-landing-page-optimization-guide)). Touch targets ≥44px, loads under 3 seconds.

## Best channels (1-2, decisive, with why — respect 2-5 hrs/week)

Pick exactly **two**. Do not spread thin.

**1. SEO + a written content hub (The Wisdom Walk), as the long-game engine.** Faith audiences *search* for prayer help ("how to pray," "what is the ACTS method," "prayer for anxiety"). Articles compound — one good post earns traffic for years with zero ongoing spend. This is the highest-leverage use of your 2–5 hrs because AI drafts and you approve. It feeds the email list on autopilot and is the most budget-friendly channel that exists.

**2. Direct pastor outreach (warm, peer-to-peer) for the Church plan.** You are a pastor. Your single most valuable channel for $49/mo accounts is *talking to other pastors* — your existing network, local ministerial associations, denominational groups, pastor Facebook groups. This isn't "marketing," it's ministry conversation: "I built a thing to help my people pray; want to see it?" One hour a week of genuine outreach can land Church accounts that no ad ever could. It also bypasses the paid-ad throttling that hits faith content.

**Why not social-first?** Social (below) matters, but as a *distribution layer* for content you're already making — not your primary engine. Organic social alone is a treadmill that eats hours and stalls without a list. SEO + peer outreach respects your time and plays to your unfair advantages (you're a pastor who can write).

## Organic content strategy (themes + cadence, fed by The Wisdom Walk where relevant)

**Hub-and-spoke:** Write long-form articles on **The Wisdom Walk** (thewisdomwalk.com) — your owned article hub — then break each into social posts and email content. Write once, publish everywhere. (This is "repurposing": one article becomes a week of posts.)

**Four content themes (rotate):**
1. **The how-to of prayer** — "What is the ACTS method?", "How to pray when you don't feel like it," "Building a 5-minute daily prayer habit." (Captures Dana + SEO.)
2. **Prayer for specific seasons** — "Prayers for anxiety," "How to pray for a prodigal child," "Praying through grief." (High-emotion, high-search, highly shareable.)
3. **The praying church (Pastor Paul track)** — "How to lead a 21-day church prayer challenge," "Why your congregation stops praying mid-week — and how to fix it." (Pre-sells the Church plan.)
4. **Built-by-a-pastor story** — occasional, honest posts on *why* you built Simply Pray. (Trust + brand voice.)

**Cadence (realistic for 2–5 hrs/wk):**
- **1 article every 2 weeks** on The Wisdom Walk (AI drafts from your outline; you edit for voice and theology). ~2 hrs.
- **3 short social posts/week**, repurposed from that article (schedule them all at once with Buffer/Metricool free). ~1 hr.
- **1 email every 2 weeks** to the growing list (your best article + a warm note). ~0.5 hr.

That's a complete content engine inside 5 hours, most of it batched.

## Paid ad strategy (only if/when appropriate; if not yet, say so + the trigger to start)

**Not yet. Do not spend on ads now.** Three reasons: (1) you have no list, no proven message, and a pre-launch product — paid traffic to an unvalidated offer just burns money; (2) faith content gets throttled and frequently rejected on Meta/Google ad platforms, so you'd fight the system on day one; (3) organic-first is your stated principle, and it's the right one.

**The trigger to start paid:** Begin small paid tests **only after all three are true** — (a) you have **at least one lead magnet + headline that converts cold visitors to email at a steady rate** (you've seen the same message win twice), (b) you have **100+ engaged email subscribers** proving the message resonates, and (c) the **app is live** so there's a real product to convert into.

**When that day comes, start here (max ~$150–$250/mo of your budget):**
- **Promote a high-performing article**, not the app — "boost" your best-converting Wisdom Walk post to a faith-interest audience, sending to the lead magnet. Content ads sail through review where product ads get flagged.
- **Pinterest** deserves a test for the prayer-guide/printable angle — it behaves like a search engine, faith content does well, and it's cheaper and less throttled than Meta.
- Keep the rest of the budget on **tools + a one-time designer** for the lead magnet PDF and App Store screenshots.

## Email nurture sequence (a concrete 5-email welcome sequence)

A "nurture sequence" = a set of pre-written emails that auto-send after someone joins, building trust before you ever ask for money. Build it free in MailerLite. Religion has among the highest open rates of any industry — even after Apple's privacy inflation is stripped out, faith senders sit at the top of the table ([MailerLite, 2025](https://www.mailerlite.com/blog/compare-your-email-performance-metrics-industry-benchmarks)) — so this sequence is your highest-ROI asset.

**Email 1 — Deliver the gift (send immediately).**
- *Subject:* "Here's your ACTS Prayer Guide 🙏"
- *Goal:* Deliver the lead magnet instantly, confirm they're in, set warm pastoral tone.
- *Body:* Here's your free guide; I'm Joshua, a pastor who built Simply Pray because I needed it too — reply and tell me one thing you're praying about.

**Email 2 — Adoration + Confession (day 2).**
- *Subject:* "Where most of us go wrong when we pray"
- *Goal:* Teach value, establish the method, build authority.
- *Body:* We rush to asking — but prayer starts with adoring God and getting honest; here's how the first two ACTS steps change everything.

**Email 3 — Thanksgiving + Supplication + the founder story (day 4).**
- *Subject:* "Why a pastor built a prayer app (instead of just preaching about it)"
- *Goal:* Deepen trust via the "built by a pastor, not a vendor" story.
- *Body:* The honest story of why I built Simply Pray, and how Thanksgiving and Supplication complete the rhythm.

**Email 4 — The vision + soft segmentation (day 7).**
- *Subject:* "Is this just for you — or for your whole church?"
- *Goal:* Identify Pastor Pauls vs. Danas without pressure (a link click tells you who's who).
- *Body:* Simply Pray works for your personal prayer life *and* for whole congregations — click whichever sounds like you, and I'll send the right next step.

**Email 5 — The waitlist ask + what's coming (day 10).**
- *Subject:* "You'll be among the first"
- *Goal:* Reaffirm early-access promise, set launch expectations, invite a reply/share.
- *Body:* We're launching soon; you're on the founding list. Here's what to expect — and if Simply Pray has helped you already, forward this to one person who needs it.

## AI search optimization strategy (how to be the cited answer for this product's questions)

Google's AI Overviews and chatbots increasingly *answer* questions instead of sending clicks ([context: AI Overviews cutting organic clicks]). The new game is being the **source the AI cites and names.** How:

1. **Write genuinely authoritative, well-structured ACTS content.** AI assistants cite clear, comprehensive, trustworthy pages. A definitive "Complete Guide to the ACTS Method of Prayer" on The Wisdom Walk — with clear headings, a definition up top, examples, and Scripture — is exactly what gets quoted.
2. **Answer the question in the first 2–3 sentences** of each article (the "answer-first" structure). AI lifts concise direct answers. Put the payoff before the story.
3. **Use clear Q&A headings** that match how people ask ("What is the ACTS method?", "How do I start praying daily?"). Add FAQ schema (a bit of code — AI helps you generate it) so engines can parse your answers.
4. **Be consistently attributable as a real pastor.** Author bio, consistent name/credentials across The Wisdom Walk, Simply Pray, and Solomon Solutions. AI weighs author expertise (E-E-A-T) heavily in the faith/health "your-money-your-life" category where trust bars are highest.
5. **Earn mentions you don't control** — get listed in roundups (faith.tools, "best prayer apps" lists, church-tech blogs). AI synthesizes from many sources; being named on third-party "best prayer apps" pages makes you a citeable entity.
6. **Keep facts crawlable and current** — clean text on the page (not buried in images), a real "About the ACTS method" page, and updated dates. When someone asks an AI "what's a good prayer app for my church," you want to be in the answer.

## Conversion metrics (the 3-5 numbers to watch + rough target benchmarks)

Watch these five. Don't drown in analytics — these tell the whole story.

1. **Landing-page → email opt-in rate.** *Target: 8–15%+.* Average waitlist/landing pages convert ~2–5%, good ones 10–15%, and a warm, well-built faith page can sit at the high end ([Waitlister, 2026](https://waitlister.me/growth-hub/guides/waitlist-landing-page-optimization-guide); [LanderLab, 2026](https://landerlab.io/blog/landing-page-conversion-rate)). If you're under ~5%, fix the headline/offer before driving more traffic.
2. **Email open rate.** *Target: 30%+ (faith runs high).* Religion is the top industry for opens; even discounting Apple's privacy inflation, aim well above the ~20–25% cross-industry "good" mark ([MailerLite, 2025](https://www.mailerlite.com/blog/compare-your-email-performance-metrics-industry-benchmarks)). Falling opens = your subject lines or trust slipped.
3. **Email click rate.** *Target: 3%+.* Whether people *act*, not just open. This predicts launch-day conversion.
4. **Waitlist size + growth rate.** *Target: 100 subscribers in 30 days, 500 in 90.* Your launch-day fuel. Track week-over-week growth more than the raw number.
5. **Church-plan leads (qualified pastor conversations).** *Target: 5–10 real conversations in 90 days, 1–3 committed at launch.* Low count, high value — one Church account ≈ 28 Personal subs. This is the number that pays the bills.

## Compliance / brand guardrails (what NOT to say for this product)

- **No guilt, shame, or fear-based copy.** Never imply someone is a bad Christian, prays wrong, or is failing God. ("You're not bad at prayer" — yes. "Real Christians pray daily" — never.) This is the line that protects trust in the faith niche.
- **No prosperity-gospel or transactional promises.** Don't promise that praying "right" yields health, wealth, answered requests, or outcomes God controls. Prayer is relationship, not a vending machine.
- **No theological overreach or denominational tribalism.** Keep it historic-Christian and broadly welcoming (ACTS spans traditions). Don't take sides that needlessly exclude.
- **No fake urgency, fake scarcity, or fake social proof.** "Be among the first" is honest (you're pre-launch). "Only 3 spots left!" or invented testimonial counts are not — and they'd torch credibility with a discerning audience.
- **Don't claim it's on the App Store until it is.** Keep "download" language *staged* and clearly future-tense ("launching soon," "reserve your spot"). The dead-link problem is the cautionary tale — never repeat it.
- **Handle congregation/member data with explicit care.** The Church plan involves member management; state plainly how data is stored and used. Pastors are guarding their flock's trust — privacy clarity is a selling point, not fine print.
- **Stay "built by a pastor, not a vendor" — but don't weaponize it.** Use your pastoral identity to build trust, never to pressure ("as your pastor, you should…"). Warm, confident, Scripture-rooted — never manipulative.
- **Use Scripture accurately and in context.** A misused verse in a faith product is a credibility wound. When in doubt, cite plainly (e.g., Romans 8:26 on the Spirit interceding when we lack words).
