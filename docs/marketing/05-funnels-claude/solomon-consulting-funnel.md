# Solomon Solutions (Church Consulting) — Funnel & Growth Plan
> Built by a pastor, not a vendor: practical systems, leadership, and AI help for churches that want to disciple people well — not just buy more software.

---

*A quick note on jargon, since marketing isn't your day job. A "funnel" is just the path a stranger walks from "never heard of you" to "booked a call." A "lead magnet" is a free, useful thing you give away in exchange for an email address. A "CTA" is a "call to action" — the one thing you want the reader to do next (here, almost always: book the free call). I define terms inline the first time they show up.*

---

## Primary audience

**Pastor Paul — the lead pastor of a small-to-mid church (75–600 attendees).**

He is the decision-maker, the budget-holder, and the person who feels the pain. He is not anti-technology; he is overwhelmed by it. He has a website nobody updates, a discipleship "process" that lives in his head, three volunteers who each own a different app, and a growing unease that AI is coming for ministry whether he's ready or not. He has been burned by a "vendor" who sold him a platform and disappeared.

What he actually wants: **clarity and trust.** He wants someone who has stood in his pulpit, knows what a Tuesday elders' meeting feels like, and can translate "technology" and "systems" into "more people actually growing in Christ." He does not want to be sold to. He responds to Scripture, to candor, and to someone who has clearly done this themselves.

Why he's the priority: he buys the consulting *and* becomes the gateway to the rest of the ecosystem — he adopts Simply Pray's Church plan, champions HopeStack to his care team, and refers other pastors (his network is other pastors). One Pastor Paul is worth far more than his single contract. The fastest revenue path is reaching pastors Joshua can already get to — warm network first.

## Secondary audience

**The "denominational connector" / executive or associate pastor at a larger church.**

This is the person one rung up or sideways from Pastor Paul — an executive pastor, a network/association leader, or a multisite operations pastor who oversees several churches. They don't just have one problem; they see the same problem across ten churches. They can't be reached with the same warmth-only approach (they vet more, they involve committees), but a single relationship here can produce multiple engagements and a speaking/workshop slot in front of a room full of Pastor Pauls.

Treat them as a force-multiplier, not the first dollar. Most early effort goes to the primary audience; secondary is where month-2-and-beyond leverage lives.

## Core offer

**The current front-door offer stays: a free 30-minute strategy call** (Calendly: grace-chapel/strategy-session). For a beginner with no audience, a free, low-pressure call is exactly right — it's the natural "next step" for a trust-driven, high-ticket, low-volume sale. Nobody buys $5K+ consulting from a button; they buy it from a conversation.

But the call needs a **named, productized offer behind it** so the conversation has somewhere to go. Right now there are no prices, which is fine to keep off the website, but you should walk into every call with **three tiered packages** ready (tiers convert better than one number because they let the buyer self-select and make the middle option feel safe):

- **Tier 1 — "Clarity Audit" (entry, fixed-fee, ~2–3 weeks):** a diagnostic of their systems, discipleship pipeline, and tech stack, ending in a written roadmap. Low risk, gets you in the door, often becomes Tier 2.
- **Tier 2 — "Systems & Discipleship Build" (core engagement, multi-month):** you actually rebuild the discipleship process, leadership/role structure, and integrate the technology.
- **Tier 3 — "Private AI for Ministry" (premium, the differentiator):** a private LLM (large language model — the kind of AI behind ChatGPT) deployed on the church's own VPS (virtual private server — a private computer in the cloud they control), so staff get AI help *without* sending congregation data to a public tool. This is the offer almost no other consultant can credibly deliver, and it's the wedge that makes Solomon Solutions distinct.

The call's job: diagnose, build trust, and recommend the right tier. The call is the conversion event; the tiers are what gets sold.

## Lead magnet ideas (3, ranked, with why each works)

A lead magnet trades something genuinely useful for an email address, so you can nurture pastors who aren't ready to book yet.

**1. "The Pastor's Guide to AI: 7 Honest Questions Before You Use It in Ministry" (PDF, ~6–8 pages).**
*Why it wins:* This is the single highest-intent topic for your exact buyer right now — pastors are adopting AI fast and are anxious about it, and almost no one is giving them a *pastoral, ethics-first* answer (everyone else is selling tools). It positions Joshua as the trustworthy guide, directly teases Tier 3 (private AI), and is shareable pastor-to-pastor. It plays to your unfair advantage (pastor + engineer). This is the one to build first.

**2. "The Healthy Church Systems Scorecard" (a self-scored checklist / mini-assessment).**
*Why it wins:* Assessments convert better than guides because the reader gets a *personalized result* ("you scored 14/30 — your discipleship pipeline is your weakest system"). That result is the natural bridge to "want help fixing your weakest area? Book a call." It surfaces the exact pain the Clarity Audit is built to solve, so it pre-sells Tier 1. Easy to build in a Google Form or a simple page.

**3. "Discipleship That Doesn't Live in Your Head: A One-Page Framework" (PDF).**
*Why it wins:* It's pure ministry value with zero tech, which makes it the safest, most shareable, most "this guy gets ministry, not just software" asset — important for the anti-vendor positioning. It's lower buying-intent than #1 and #2 (it doesn't point as directly at a paid engagement), which is why it ranks third, but it's excellent fuel for The Wisdom Walk and for building list trust over time.

## Best channels (1-2, decisive, with why — respect 2-5 hrs/week)

With no list, no audience, ~$1,000/mo, and 2–5 hrs/week, you do **two things only.** Everything else is a distraction right now.

**Channel 1 (the revenue channel): direct warm outreach to pastors Joshua can already reach.** Personal email and one-to-one messages to pastors in his existing network — former colleagues, denominational contacts, seminary connections, friends-of-friends. This is the fastest path to the first paying client because the trust already exists; you're not building an audience, you're activating a relationship. Religion has the highest email engagement of any industry (the religion sector averages a **55.7% email open rate** vs. ~43% overall [Source: MailerLite 2025 benchmarks](https://www.mailerlite.com/blog/compare-your-email-performance-metrics-industry-benchmarks)), and one-to-one notes beat any blast. This is where the first dollars come from in 30 days.

**Channel 2 (the compounding channel): long-form written content on The Wisdom Walk, repurposed to LinkedIn.** One genuinely useful article per week, written for Pastor Paul, that also feeds SEO and AI-search citations. LinkedIn (not Instagram/TikTok) because that's where pastors and church leaders behave professionally, faith content isn't throttled the way it is in paid social, and text posts cost nothing. This is slow but it's the asset that keeps working while you sleep.

Why not paid ads, Instagram, YouTube, a podcast, etc.: they all demand hours or budget you don't have yet, and none of them is proven for your message. Prove the message in conversations and writing first.

**Cadence (fits 2–5 hrs/week):** one anchor article per week on **The Wisdom Walk (thewisdomwalk.com)**, drafted by AI and approved by Joshua, then sliced into **2–3 short LinkedIn posts** pulled straight from that article. Write once, distribute three times. That's the whole content engine.

**Four rotating themes (one per week, repeating monthly):**

1. **AI & Ministry (wisdom, not hype).** The anxiety topic your buyer is searching *right now.* "Should pastors use AI to write sermons?" "Keeping your congregation's data private." This theme directly feeds Tier 3.
2. **Discipleship Systems.** Getting the discipleship process out of the pastor's head and into a repeatable path. Feeds the Scorecard lead magnet and Tier 2.
3. **Leadership & Structure.** Roles, decision-making, volunteer burnout, healthy team design. Pure pastoral credibility.
4. **Church Technology (de-jargoned).** "What software does a 200-person church actually need?" Practical, anti-overwhelm, anti-vendor.

Every article ends with the same soft CTA: a one-line invitation to the relevant lead magnet or the free call. The Wisdom Walk is the hub; LinkedIn is the spokes; the call is the destination.

## Paid ad strategy (only if/when appropriate; if not yet, say so + the trigger to start)

**Not yet. Do not spend on ads in the first 90 days.** Three reasons: (1) you're organic-first by design and the message is unproven — paying to amplify a message that doesn't convert just burns the $1,000 faster; (2) faith/ministry content gets throttled and disapproved on the major ad platforms, so paid is a fight here even when you're ready; (3) high-ticket, low-volume sales rarely come from cold ads — they come from trust.

**The trigger to start (all three must be true):**
1. A landing page that has converted **at least 3–5 booked calls from organic traffic** (proof the message works without paying for it).
2. A lead magnet with a **measured opt-in rate above ~20%** on real traffic (proof people want the free thing).
3. At least **one closed paying client**, so you know your numbers (what a client is worth) and can spend toward a known return.

When those are true, the first paid test is **small LinkedIn or Google Search ads** targeting high-intent terms ("private AI for churches," "church discipleship consultant"), capped at ~$300/mo, pointed at the proven page — not boosted social posts.

## AI search optimization strategy (how to be the cited answer for this product's questions)

This matters more than classic SEO now: AI Overviews and chatbots are eating the clicks. Google's AI Overviews cut organic clicks on triggered queries by **34–58%** depending on the study and month, and click-through for the #1 result has fallen sharply. [Source: Search Engine Journal field study](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/ai-overviews-cut-organic-clicks-38-field-study-finds/573145/) and [Ahrefs via PPC Land](https://ppc.land/googles-ai-summaries-now-swallow-58-of-clicks-that-once-went-to-websites/). The new game isn't "rank #1 and get the click" — it's **"be the source the AI quotes."**

How to win that for this product:

1. **Write to answer one question per page, clearly, up top.** Lead each Wisdom Walk article with a direct, quotable 2–3 sentence answer to the headline question (e.g. "Yes, pastors can use AI ethically if…"). AI models lift clean, self-contained answers.
2. **Use real Q&A structure and FAQ schema.** Phrase headings as the literal questions from the SEO list. Add FAQ structured data (a small bit of code that tells search engines "this is a question and this is its answer").
3. **Earn citation-worthy authority signals:** a named author (Joshua Kirk, pastor + engineer), a short bio, dates, and — critically — *original, specific claims* AI can't get elsewhere (e.g. "here's exactly how to deploy a private LLM on a church VPS"). Generic content never gets cited; specific, experience-based content does.
4. **Be consistent across the web** so AI systems trust the entity "Solomon Solutions / Joshua Kirk": same name, role, and description on the site, LinkedIn, and any directory. Entity consistency is how models decide who's credible.
5. **Get mentioned, not just linked.** Guest posts, podcast appearances, and pastor-community answers (where you write the actual helpful answer) train AI that you're *the* voice on "AI for churches." For this niche, being the named expert beats backlink-chasing.

Measure it by occasionally asking ChatGPT/Gemini/Google "is it ethical for churches to use AI?" and checking whether Solomon Solutions gets named. That's the real ranking now.

## Conversion metrics (the 3-5 numbers to watch + rough target benchmarks)

Keep it to a handful you'll actually look at (GA4 + Search Console + MailerLite + Calendly, all free):

1. **Landing page → booked call conversion.** Target **2–5%** of visitors book. B2B professional-services pages typically convert at 1–3% on consultation requests; aim for the top of that range given warm traffic. [Source: First Page Sage 2026](https://firstpagesage.com/seo-blog/b2b-landing-page-conversion-rates/)
2. **Lead-magnet opt-in rate.** Target **20–35%** of landing-page visitors who don't book at least grab the free guide.
3. **Email open rate (nurture).** Target **45%+** — realistic and even conservative for this niche (religion averages ~55%). [Source: MailerLite 2025](https://www.mailerlite.com/blog/compare-your-email-performance-metrics-industry-benchmarks). If you're below 40%, the subject lines or list quality need work.
4. **Booked-call → paying-client close rate.** Target **20–30%** of strategy calls turn into a paid engagement (audit or build). High-ticket trust sales close well when the call is consultative, not a pitch.
5. **Cost per booked call.** In the organic phase this is ~$0 in cash and a few hours of time; once you add paid, this becomes the number that decides whether to scale. Watch it the moment any spend starts.

The North Star for the first 90 days is dead simple: **booked calls per month**, then **clients closed.**

## Compliance / brand guardrails (what NOT to say for this product)

This is a trust-driven, faith niche. The fastest way to lose is to sound like everything pastors distrust.

- **No fear, guilt, or manipulation.** No "your church is failing if you don't do this," no false scarcity ("only 2 spots!"), no countdown-timer pressure. Pastors smell it instantly and it violates the brand.
- **Never "vendor" language.** Don't lead with features, platforms, or "solutions packages." Lead with ministry outcomes and the pastor's reality. The whole brand is "pastor, not vendor."
- **Don't promise spiritual or numerical results you can't control.** No "we'll grow your church," no "guaranteed more conversions/baptisms." You guide systems and stewardship; God grows the church. Promise clarity, structure, and wise tools — not outcomes only the Spirit owns.
- **Handle AI honestly.** Never imply AI replaces the pastor, the Spirit, or genuine discipleship. Always frame AI as a tool used wisely, with the privacy/ethics caveats front and center — that honesty *is* the selling point. Don't overhype capabilities.
- **Protect congregation data in how you talk about it.** Don't encourage pasting sensitive pastoral/counseling info into public AI tools; that concern is the reason Tier 3 exists.
- **Scripture with care, not as decoration.** Use it where it genuinely anchors the point (wisdom, stewardship, shepherding). Don't proof-text to sell. Misused Scripture costs more trust than it earns.
- **Stay non-partisan and non-divisive.** No political, denominational-tribal, or culture-war framing. The audience spans traditions; the brand serves the Church, broadly.
- **No deceptive claims or fake social proof.** Until you have real testimonials, don't invent them. In this niche, integrity is the product.
