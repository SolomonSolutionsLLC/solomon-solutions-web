# Fast validation strategies for early-stage products

> Cheap, fast ways to find out whether real people actually want (and will pay for) your product *before* you spend months building or marketing it.

## Beginner explanation

"Validation" just means **getting proof that real people have the problem you think they have, and that they'd actually use or pay for your solution.** It is the opposite of guessing.

A few plain-English terms you'll keep seeing:

- **MVP (Minimum Viable Product):** the smallest version of your product that can teach you something real. "Minimum" = least work; "viable" = still useful enough that someone could try it. An MVP can even be a single web page, not working software.
- **Problem/solution fit:** proof that (a) the problem is real and painful, and (b) people believe your solution would fix it. This comes *before* you've built much.
- **The Mom Test:** a way to talk to people so they tell you the truth instead of being polite. Named because even your mom will lie to you if you ask "Do you like my idea?" The fix: ask about their *actual past behavior*, never about your idea. Bad question: "Would you use a prayer app?" Good question: "Walk me through the last time you tried to keep a daily prayer habit. What did you use? Where did it break down?" ([The Mom Test summary](https://www.sachinrekhi.com/p/the-mom-test-rob-fitzpatrick))
- **Smoke test:** put up a simple landing page describing the product as if it exists, drive a little traffic, and count how many people click "Join the waitlist" or "Notify me." Their click is a vote with real intent. ([Smoke test guide](https://painonsocial.com/blog/smoke-tests-complete-guide-startup-founders))
- **Waitlist:** an email sign-up for a product that isn't out yet. It both measures demand and gives you a launch-day audience.
- **Design partners:** a tiny group (usually 5-12) of real target customers who help you shape the product before it exists, in exchange for early access and a discount. ([Bessemer, 2026](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/design-partners-the-pre-launch-edge-most-ai-founders-ignore))
- **Pre-selling:** asking for money (a deposit, a discounted annual plan, a paid pilot) *before* the product is finished. A signed check is the strongest validation there is.
- **Concierge MVP:** you deliver the service by hand, manually, with no real software, and the customer knows it. You learn exactly what to build by doing it yourself first. ([Concierge MVP](https://learningloop.io/plays/concierge))

The golden rule: **a "yes, that sounds great" is worth nothing. A click, an email, a calendar booking, or a payment is worth a lot.** Always chase behavior, not compliments.

## Why it matters

The most common reason new products die is not bad code or bad marketing. It's building something not enough people wanted. CB Insights' analysis of failed startups found **42% failed because of "no market need,"** and their 2024 refresh of 431 recently-shut-down companies put **poor product-market fit at 43%** ([CB Insights](https://www.cbinsights.com/research/report/startup-failure-reasons-top/); [2024 update](https://preuve.ai/blog/why-startups-fail-market-fit)).

For you specifically, validation protects your two scarcest resources: **$1,000/month and 2-5 hours/week.** A smoke-test page costs near-zero and tells you in two weeks what a six-month build would tell you the hard way. And because you're organic-first, validation *is* your marketing: the same landing page, interviews, and waitlist that prove demand also become your launch audience.

For pre-launch products (Simply Pray, HopeStack), the point of these years is to learn cheaply, not to look finished.

## How it applies to YOUR products

Run validation in priority order, reusing The Wisdom Walk as your trust engine.

**Simply Pray (priority, pre-launch iOS app).** You can't sell on the App Store yet, so validate the *message and the demand* now. Put a one-page smoke test on simplypray.io: explain the ACTS prayer method, show 2-3 app mockups, and offer "Get early access" (email waitlist). Aim for a **10%+ sign-up rate** from visitors who arrive with intent; under 5% means rework the message before you spend a dollar on ads ([Smoke test benchmark](https://founderfaqs.com/blogs/how-to-run-a-smoke-test-landing-page-to-prove-demand)). The hardest, most valuable signal is the **$49/mo Church plan**: do Mom Test calls with 8-10 Pastor Pauls about how their congregation currently prays together. This is organic and free.

**HopeStack (counseling-center CRM, beta + waitlist).** This is your highest revenue-per-customer product, which makes **design partners** the right play. Recruit 5-12 Care Director Carols as design partners now: free/discounted access in exchange for weekly feedback and a **hard conversion deadline** ("at beta end, go paid or not") ([Bessemer](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/design-partners-the-pre-launch-edge-most-ai-founders-ignore)). Because it's HIPAA-aligned, validate *workflow and trust*, not just features. Three paying design partners beats 300 newsletter subscribers here.

**Heartward (AI theological Q&A, $5/$12/$25).** Low price, consumer audience, so validate with a **fake-door / smoke test**: a landing page with the three tiers and a "Notify me" button, plus a tiny manual (concierge) version where you answer a few real questions by hand to see what people actually ask. Use The Wisdom Walk articles to drive free traffic.

**Consulting / Solomon Solutions (high-ticket B2B).** Already validated by the offer itself: the **free 30-min strategy call** is your Mom Test interview *and* your sales call. Every call teaches you which church problems are most painful, feeding all the other products.

**The Wisdom Walk (content/SEO hub).** Validate *topics* before writing deeply: publish short posts, see which get read and emailed, and let the winners decide your roadmap. It's the organic top of funnel for everything above.

Keep paid spend off until a free smoke test clears 10%; then spend a slice of the $1k to confirm the result with strangers.

## Examples

- **Mom Test call, Pastor Paul:** Instead of "Would your church pay $49/mo for a prayer app?" you ask, "Last month, how did people in your congregation share prayer requests? Walk me through it." If he describes a messy group text and a forgotten Google Form, that's a real, painful problem. If he shrugs, you just saved yourself a build.
- **Simply Pray smoke test:** A single page, the ACTS method explained, three phone mockups, and "Join 0 → first 100 in early access." Share it in two pastor Facebook groups and your church network (organic). 200 visits, 30 sign-ups = 15% = strong signal to keep going.
- **HopeStack design partner:** A nearby Christian counseling center uses HopeStack free for 60 days. You sit with Carol weekly. At day 60 you say, "Plan is $X/mo starting next month, or we part friends." Her "yes" is worth more than any survey.
- **Heartward concierge:** Before automating, you personally answer 25 theology questions submitted through a form, timing yourself and noting patterns. You learn the real questions Seeker Sam and Devout Dana ask, and whether they'd pay.
- **Pre-sell a consulting pilot:** Offer one church a discounted "private AI deployment pilot" at a fixed price, paid up front. Cash now = validation now.

## Common mistakes

- **Asking for compliments, not facts.** "Do you like this?" invites politeness. Beware the three bad signals: compliments, hypotheticals ("I would totally..."), and wishlists ([The Mom Test](https://blog.uxtweak.com/the-mom-test/)).
- **Building before talking.** As an engineer, code is your comfort zone. Resist it. Ten conversations and a landing page beat ten thousand lines of code at this stage.
- **Treating sign-ups as revenue.** A waitlist proves curiosity, not willingness to pay. For HopeStack and the Church plan, push toward a paid commitment.
- **Vanity audience size.** 1,000 newsletter readers who never convert is worse than 5 paying design partners.
- **Launching too loud, too early.** You only need a small sample to read demand; skip big campaigns until the message is proven ([MVP guide](https://www.netsolutions.com/hub/minimum-viable-product/launch/)).
- **Faith-niche trust mistakes:** over-promising ("AI that answers all your spiritual questions"), looking salesy in ministry spaces, or implying endorsement you don't have. Trust is your moat; protect it. For HopeStack, never cut corners on HIPAA language even in a test.
- **Spamming pastor groups.** Cold-dropping links erodes the very trust you're building. Lead with help (a Wisdom Walk article), then invite.

## First 3 actions for Joshua

1. **This week (2 hours): Run 5 Mom Test calls.** Pick your top product (Simply Pray's Church plan or HopeStack). Use Calendly to book 5 Pastor Pauls or Care Director Carols from your existing church network. Ask only about their past behavior and current workarounds. Write down exact quotes, not your interpretations.
2. **Next week (2-3 hours): Ship one smoke-test page.** Build a single Card/Vercel page for Simply Pray on simplypray.io with the ACTS method, 3 mockups, and an email waitlist. Add GA4. Share it organically in 2-3 trusted pastor groups and your network. Target 10%+ sign-up.
3. **Within 30 days (2-3 hours): Recruit 3 HopeStack design partners with a deadline.** Offer 3 counseling centers free 60-day access in exchange for weekly feedback, ending in a binary "go paid or not" ask. Three real conversions tell you more than any survey.
