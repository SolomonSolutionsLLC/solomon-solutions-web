# Paid ads basics

> Paying a platform (Google, Meta, Apple, YouTube) to put your message in front of people who haven't found you yet — the rented opposite of free, "organic" reach.

## Beginner explanation

Paid ads means you give a platform money, and in return it shows your ad to people. You don't pay for the ad to exist; you pay each time it gets seen or clicked. That's the whole model.

A handful of terms cover almost everything:

- **Impression** = one time your ad is shown on a screen.
- **CPM** (cost per *mille*, Latin for thousand) = what you pay per 1,000 impressions. This is how you "rent eyeballs." Meta runs roughly $7–$18 CPM depending on industry ([AdAmigo, 2025](https://www.adamigo.ai/blog/meta-ads-benchmarks-2025-by-industry)).
- **Click** = someone taps your ad.
- **CPC** (cost per click) = what you pay per click. Google Search averaged **$5.26** in 2025; Meta averaged about **$1.72** ([WordStream, updated May 2026](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/2025-google-ads-benchmarks); [AdAmigo, 2025](https://www.adamigo.ai/blog/meta-ads-benchmarks-2025-by-industry)).
- **CTR** (click-through rate) = clicks ÷ impressions, as a percent. It tells you whether the ad is interesting. Google Search averages ~6.66%; Meta is far lower at ~0.90% (people scroll feeds, they don't search) ([WordStream](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/2025-google-ads-benchmarks); [AdAmigo](https://www.adamigo.ai/blog/meta-ads-benchmarks-2025-by-industry)).
- **Conversion** = the action you actually want (a strategy-call booking, an app install, a waitlist signup). **Conversion rate** = conversions ÷ clicks.
- **CAC** (customer acquisition cost) = total ad spend ÷ new customers. If you spend $1,000 and get 10 paying customers, your CAC is $100.
- **ROAS** (return on ad spend) = revenue ÷ ad spend. $30 earned per $10 spent is a 3:1 ROAS ([HubSpot glossary](https://www.hubspot.com/glossary/return-on-ad-spend)).

Two more ideas. **Targeting** is telling the platform *who* should see the ad — by keyword (Google), or by age, location, and interests (Meta). **Retargeting** is showing ads only to people who already visited your site or app; they know you, so they convert more cheaply.

The core difference between platforms: **Google catches existing demand** (someone is already searching "counseling center software"), while **Meta creates demand** (someone wasn't looking, but the post stops their scroll).

## Why it matters

Organic reach (free content, SEO, word of mouth) is slow and you don't control the dial. Paid ads are the only marketing lever where you spend $1 today and can measure exactly what came back tomorrow — every impression, click, and conversion is tracked. That makes ads a *testing machine*, not just a megaphone: you can learn which message and which audience actually responds before you bet a year of content on it.

Costs are rising — Google CPCs jumped ~13% year over year — but so is performance, with 65% of industries posting better conversion rates in 2025 ([WordStream](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/2025-google-ads-benchmarks)). The takeaway for a beginner: ads reward the person who measures, not the person who spends the most.

## How it applies to YOUR products

Given $1,000/month, organic-first, no audience yet, and 2–5 hrs/week, **ads are a small, late-stage tool — not your engine.** Prove the message free first; then use ads to pour fuel on what already works.

- **The Wisdom Walk (your trust hub):** Spend nothing here yet. Win on free SEO first. Ads on a brand-new site send paid traffic to a page nobody trusts, which wastes money and teaches you little.
- **Simply Pray (priority, pre-launch):** This is your *best* ad candidate, but **only at launch, not now.** While pre-launch, run a tiny **Meta** test ($5–$10/day) driving to a waitlist landing page. Meta's low CPC makes cheap audience-and-message testing possible. Hold **Apple Search Ads** until you're live on the App Store — that's where it shines (median cost per tap ~$0.92; average cost per acquisition ~$2.51 in 2025, [MobileAction, 2026](https://www.mobileaction.co/report/apple-ads-2026-benchmark-report/cpa-search-results-ads/)).
- **Consulting / Solomon Solutions (high-ticket B2B):** One closed church client can pay for months of ads, so even a $5.26 Google click is fine here. But your offer is a *free 30-min call* sold on **trust** — pastors buy from people they believe. Founder-brand organic content will outperform cold ads until you have proof. Reserve a small **Google Search** test for high-intent terms ("church management consultant," "private AI for churches") later.
- **HopeStack (highest revenue per customer, B2B SaaS):** Highest ARPU = highest tolerable CAC, so ads make the most *economic* sense here eventually. But it's beta/waitlist and HIPAA-sensitive; lead with content and direct outreach to counseling directors now. Save ad budget for when the product is sales-ready.
- **Heartward (low-ARPU consumer):** Lowest priority for paid. At $5–$25/month, your CAC has to be tiny or you lose money on every acquisition. Let organic and the cross-product ecosystem feed it.

Faith-niche reality: **Google restricts religious targeting.** You cannot use customer-match lists, lookalike/similar audiences, or audience expansion for religious content; you're limited to Google's predefined in-market, affinity, demographic, and contextual targeting ([Google Ads Policy, 2026](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/16701958?hl=en)). Faith-app ads have been outright rejected even after removing words like "Christian" and "Bible" ([Fox Business, 2025](https://www.foxbusiness.com/politics/christian-kids-app-claims-google-tiktok-blocked-ads-congress-pressed-act-its-devastating)). Plan for friction.

## Examples

- **Simply Pray waitlist test (Meta, $7/day = ~$210/mo):** Audience = parents and adults 28–55 interested in faith/wellness. Ad: "A simpler way to pray, built on the ACTS method. Join the early-access list." Goal = email signups. If you get signups under ~$3 each, your message works and you scale at launch. If clicks are pricey and nobody signs up, you just saved yourself a bad launch.
- **Consulting (Google Search, later, $15/day):** Bid on "church management consultant." Pastor Paul searches it, lands on a page offering a free 30-min strategy call. One booked call that becomes a $5k engagement is a massive ROAS even at $8/click.
- **Retargeting (Meta, ~$3/day):** Anyone who reads a Wisdom Walk article but doesn't sign up later sees: "Liked that? Get the next one." Cheap, warm, and policy-safer than cold faith targeting.

## Common mistakes

- **Running ads before the message is proven.** Paid traffic to a page that doesn't convert just buys you bad news faster. Organic-first exists for this reason.
- **Spreading $1,000 across five platforms and ten products.** You'll learn nothing from any of them. Pick one product, one platform, one offer.
- **Judging too soon or never.** Give a test ~30 days and a real sample of clicks before deciding; also *do* decide — don't let a losing ad quietly drain the card.
- **Boosting posts instead of using the real ad manager.** The "Boost" button is the worst-converting, least-controllable way to spend.
- **No conversion tracking.** If you can't see signups/bookings, you're flying blind — install tracking *before* spending a dollar.
- **Faith-niche traps:** sending people to Stripe before trust is built; ignoring Google's religious-targeting limits and getting accounts flagged; using urgent/"salvation" language that ad reviewers reject. Lead with service ("a simpler way to pray"), not conversion-of-souls language.

## Recommended tools

| Tool | Rough monthly cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Meta Ads Manager | Free tool; you set spend | Best first platform — cheapest testing; free to use |
| Google Ads | Free tool; you set spend | High-intent search; mind religious-targeting limits |
| Apple Search Ads | Free tool; you set spend | Hold until Simply Pray is live in App Store |
| Google Analytics 4 (GA4) | Free | Measures what visitors do; essential |
| Meta Pixel + Google Tag | Free | Conversion tracking; install before spending |
| Landing page (Carrd/Framer) | Free–$19 | Simple waitlist/booking page to send ads to |
| Email capture (MailerLite) | Free to 1,000 contacts | Catch waitlist signups so spend isn't wasted |

You do not need a paid "ad spy" or management subscription yet. Every platform tool above is free; you only pay the ad spend itself.

## First 3 actions for Joshua

1. **Don't launch a campaign yet — set up measurement (1 hr).** Stand up one simple Simply Pray waitlist landing page and install GA4 + the Meta Pixel. Without tracking, ads teach you nothing.
2. **Build one $5–$7/day Meta waitlist test for Simply Pray (1–2 hrs).** One audience, one image, one line of copy, goal = email signup. Let AI draft three copy variants; you approve. Cap total spend at ~$150 for the month.
3. **Read your numbers after 30 days, decide once (30 min).** Compare cost-per-signup against ~$3. Under it, keep and plan to scale at App Store launch. Over it, change the *message* (not the budget) and retest. Keep consulting/HopeStack/Heartward on organic until Simply Pray proves the playbook.
