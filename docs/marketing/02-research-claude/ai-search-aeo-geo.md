# AEO / GEO / AI search optimization (getting cited by ChatGPT, Claude, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity)

> Making your website the source that AI assistants quote and link to when someone asks them a question.

## Beginner explanation

For 20 years, "getting found online" meant Google's classic list of blue links. You optimized for that with **SEO** (Search Engine Optimization) — the craft of getting your page to rank high in that list.

Now millions of people skip the list. They ask ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, or Google's new "AI Overview" (the AI-written paragraph at the top of Google results) a question and read the answer the AI writes for them. The AI often names its sources and links to a few websites. The whole game is now: **be one of the websites the AI pulls from and names.**

Two terms describe this new craft. They overlap heavily, and many practitioners treat them as the same thing ([Profound, 2025](https://www.tryprofound.com/blog/aeo-vs-geo)):

- **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)** = optimizing your content so an AI can easily *find, extract, and quote a clean answer* from it. Think: giving a direct, self-contained answer to a specific question.
- **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)** = the broader practice of shaping your whole online presence so generative AI systems *trust and reuse* your content. ("Generative" just means AI that writes original text rather than returning a list.)

A simple way to hold it: AEO makes you *extractable*; GEO makes you *trusted and reusable*.

**How does an AI decide whom to cite?** Two mechanisms. (1) Some answers come from what the model "memorized" during training — older, encyclopedic knowledge. (2) For fresh or specific questions, the AI does a live web search, pulls back several pages, and writes its answer from those. Studies of citation behavior show each platform leans differently: ChatGPT leans on Wikipedia (its single most-cited source, ~7.8% of citations) and authoritative references; Perplexity leans heavily on community discussion like Reddit (~6.6%); Google AI Overviews spread across Reddit, YouTube, and Quora ([Profound, June 2025](https://www.tryprofound.com/blog/ai-platform-citation-patterns)). Overlap between platforms is small — one analysis found only 11% of cited domains appear on both ChatGPT and Perplexity ([The Digital Bloom, 2025](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)).

**Schema / structured data** comes up constantly. Schema is invisible code (usually "JSON-LD") you add to a page that labels what things are — "this is a FAQ," "this is an author," "this is a how-to step." It does not make the AI quote you word-for-word, but it makes your content easier for crawlers to understand, which raises the odds you get pulled in ([Search Engine Land, 2025](https://searchengineland.com/schema-markup-ai-search-no-hype-472339)).

## Why it matters

The audience is enormous and growing. ChatGPT reached roughly 900 million weekly users by early 2026, up from 400 million a year earlier, and Google's AI Overviews reach more than 2 billion monthly users ([SQ Magazine, 2026](https://sqmagazine.co.uk/chatgpt-vs-google-gemini-statistics/)). AI referral traffic to websites is rising fast — ChatGPT referrals grew 52% year over year into late 2025 ([Digiday, 2025](https://digiday.com/media/in-graphic-detail-the-state-of-ai-referral-traffic-in-2025/)).

There is real evidence the tactics work. The first peer-reviewed academic study on this (Princeton, Georgia Tech, Allen Institute for AI, IIT Delhi) found that adding **statistics, citations, and direct quotations** to content boosted visibility in AI answers by up to 40% ([Princeton GEO study, 2024](https://collaborate.princeton.edu/en/publications/geo-generative-engine-optimization/)).

For Joshua specifically: this is the most budget-friendly channel that exists right now. It is fundamentally *organic* — you earn citations by publishing genuinely helpful, trustworthy content, not by buying ads. That fits an organic-first, ~$1,000/month, no-existing-audience situation better than almost anything else. A new brand with zero email list can still get quoted by ChatGPT if the page answers the question well.

## How it applies to YOUR products

**The Wisdom Walk (your content hub) is the engine.** This is where AEO/GEO lives. Every product question a pastor, care director, or seeker might type into ChatGPT becomes an article here, written to be the cleanest answer on the internet. The Wisdom Walk earns the citations; the articles point to the products. Given your 2-5 hr/week, let AI draft, you approve, publish 1-2 deep articles a month.

**Simply Pray (priority, pre-launch).** Since it is not yet on the App Store, you cannot win "best prayer app" comparison answers yet. So aim upstream at the *problem*, not the product: target questions like "What is the ACTS method of prayer?", "How do I build a daily prayer habit?", "How can my church pray together?" Answer them definitively on The Wisdom Walk, mention Simply Pray and a waitlist link inside. When the app launches, you already own the surrounding answers.

**HopeStack (counseling-center CRM, highest revenue-per-customer).** Care Director Carol asks AI things like "HIPAA-compliant CRM for a Christian counseling center" or "how to manage counseling intake and notes." These are low-volume, high-value queries — perfect for AEO because few sites answer them well. A handful of precise comparison/explainer pages could surface you to exactly the right buyer.

**Heartward / HeartwardChat (AI theological Q&A).** This product *is* an answer engine for faith questions, so the content strategy doubles as marketing: publish authoritative, well-cited articles on common theological questions Seeker Sam and Devout Dana ask ("What does the Bible say about anxiety?"). Strong sourcing here both earns AI citations and demonstrates the product's value.

**Solomon Solutions consulting (high-ticket B2B).** Pastor Paul asks "how should a church deploy AI privately?" or "church management systems tech integration." Your founder story — "built by a pastor, engineered for the church" — is a trust asset AI rewards. Offer the free 30-min strategy call as the call-to-action inside these answers.

Budget note: the writing labor is your AI drafting time. Spend cash only on one tracking tool plus your existing hosting (see table). Everything else is organic effort.

## Examples

- **A direct-answer block (AEO).** On a Wisdom Walk article titled "What is the ACTS prayer method?", open with a 40-60 word answer: "The ACTS method is a four-part prayer framework: Adoration (praising God), Confession (admitting sin), Thanksgiving (expressing gratitude), and Supplication (asking for needs). It gives structure to prayer and is widely taught in churches." That tight, extractable paragraph is exactly what an AI lifts. Simply Pray is mentioned just below as a tool that guides you through ACTS.

- **A statistic + citation (GEO).** Instead of "many people struggle to pray consistently," write "A 2023 Barna study found X% of churchgoers pray daily ([source])." The Princeton research showed adding real stats and citations is one of the strongest visibility boosts.

- **A faith-niche comparison page (HopeStack).** "HIPAA-aligned CRMs for Christian counseling centers: what to look for" — a genuinely useful, even-handed guide that happens to position HopeStack well. This is the kind of specific, low-competition query AI loves to cite.

- **An author/trust signal.** Add a real author box: "Joshua Kirk, pastor and software engineer." This feeds the E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) that AI and search both reward — and in a trust-driven faith niche, it matters double.

## Common mistakes

- **Burying the answer.** A 600-word windup before the actual answer means the AI can't extract it. Lead with the direct answer, then expand.
- **Empty schema.** Adding bare-bones FAQ schema with no real content can underperform having none at all — one analysis found rich, fully-populated schema cited 61.7% of the time versus minimal schema doing worse than no schema ([Frase, 2025/2026](https://www.frase.io/blog/faq-schema-ai-search-geo-aeo)). Populate it fully or skip it.
- **Treating it as a one-time trick.** Citations shift constantly and differ per platform; this is ongoing publishing, not a setting you flip.
- **Faith-niche over-claiming.** Avoid unverifiable spiritual or clinical promises ("this app will heal your anxiety"). AI down-weights untrustworthy claims, and your niche is compliance-sensitive (especially anything touching HopeStack's counseling/HIPAA world). Cite Scripture and reputable sources; never overstate.
- **Chasing all five products at once.** With 2-5 hr/week, spreading thin produces nothing citable. Concentrate on Simply Pray-adjacent and high-value HopeStack questions first.
- **Ignoring the basics.** AEO is not separate from a fast, crawlable, well-titled site. If your page can't be crawled, no amount of clever answering helps.

## Recommended tools

| Tool | What it does | Rough monthly cost |
|---|---|---|
| Google Search Console | Free; shows what queries find you, plus crawl/index health | Free |
| Schema markup (JSON-LD by hand or a WP/site plugin) | Adds FAQ/Article/Author structured data | Free (or free plugin) |
| Otterly.ai or Profound (entry tier) | Tracks whether AI engines mention/cite your brand | Free trial; ~$29-99/mo |
| Frase or Surfer (AEO content briefs) | Helps draft answer-optimized articles | ~$15-45/mo |
| ChatGPT / Claude (you likely already pay) | Drafts articles you approve | ~$20/mo each |
| Bing Webmaster Tools | Free; Bing's index feeds ChatGPT's live web search | Free |

Start free (Search Console + Bing Webmaster + manual schema + your existing AI subscriptions). Add one paid tracker (~$30/mo) only once you have articles live to measure. This keeps you far under $1,000/month.

## First 3 actions for Joshua

1. **Pick 5 questions and publish 1 answer this week (≈2 hrs).** Choose 5 real questions your personas type into AI (start with Simply Pray-adjacent, e.g., "What is the ACTS prayer method?"). Have AI draft one Wisdom Walk article; you edit for truth and voice. Open with a 40-60 word direct answer, add one real statistic with a citation, and link to the relevant product/waitlist.

2. **Set up free measurement (≈30 min).** Verify The Wisdom Walk (and each product site) in Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools. This is your free dashboard for what AI and search can see.

3. **Add schema + an author box (≈1 hr).** On that first article, add fully-populated FAQ and Article schema and a real "Joshua Kirk, pastor & software engineer" author block. Then check back in ~4 weeks by literally asking ChatGPT and Perplexity your 5 questions to see whether you're cited yet, and repeat the cycle monthly.
