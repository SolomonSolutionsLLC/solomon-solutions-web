# Which platforms fit each Solomon Solutions product

> A plain-English guide to picking the 1-2 marketing "platforms" (places you publish) that fit each of your products best — so you stop guessing and start posting where your people actually are.

## Beginner explanation

A "platform" here means a place where you publish content and where people find you. The big ones break into a few families:

- **Search platforms** — Google and YouTube. People type a question and find your answer. This is called **SEO** (Search Engine Optimization), which just means writing pages so Google shows them. Slow to start, but it compounds: one good article keeps bringing visitors for years.
- **Professional network** — LinkedIn. Where business decision-makers (like pastors running a church, or counseling-center directors) hang out and read. Best when *you personally* post, not a faceless company page.
- **Social feeds** — Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, Pinterest. Scrolling, images, short video. Good for reaching everyday people and building a following from zero.
- **Communities** — Reddit, Facebook Groups, Discord, niche forums. Real people asking real questions. Great for pre-launch products because you can talk *with* humans, not broadcast *at* them.
- **Email** — your list of subscribers. Not flashy, but you own it (an algorithm can't take it away), and it has the highest return of any channel.
- **App Store / podcasts** — distribution channels specific to apps and audio.

"Platform fit" means matching the *right place* to the *right product and person*. A church-software buyer behaves nothing like someone curious about faith at 11pm — so they don't live on the same platform. Trying to be everywhere is the #1 beginner trap. With 2-5 hours a week, you pick **one or two per product**, do them well, and ignore the rest.

## Why it matters

Picking wrong wastes your scarcest resource — time — and money you don't have to waste. The research is blunt about this: the most common founder mistake is "trying to be everywhere... dabbling in six channels and mastering none, resulting in shallow traction" ([First Page Sage / MarketerHire, 2026](https://marketerhire.com/blog/b2b-saas-marketing-channels)).

The payoff for choosing well is large. For business software, **SEO returns about 748%** and keeps producing leads after you stop paying, and **SEO leads close at 14.6% versus 1.7% for cold outreach** ([First Page Sage, May 2026](https://firstpagesage.com/seo-blog/b2b-saas-marketing-channels-compared/)). Organic content costs roughly **62% less than outbound and generates 3x more leads** ([MarketerHire, 2026](https://marketerhire.com/blog/b2b-saas-marketing-channels)). That is the "prove it free, then pay" model you already want.

For your faith niche specifically, trust beats reach. Consistent visual storytelling on Instagram drives "45% higher engagement," and WhatsApp/messaging hits up to 80% engagement — far above email ([Social Champ, 2025](https://www.socialchamp.com/blog/social-media-for-churches/)). And on LinkedIn, **personal posts from a real person get up to 4x the engagement of company-page posts** ([Averi.ai, 2026](https://www.averi.ai/how-to/linkedin-marketing-for-b2b-saas-the-complete-strategy-guide-for-2026)). For a pastor-engineer building a founder brand, that's your superpower.

## How it applies to YOUR products

You have one content engine (The Wisdom Walk) feeding five offers, on $1,000/month and 2-5 hours/week, organic-first, with no audience yet. Here is the decisive map.

| Product | Who you're reaching | Pick these 1-2 platforms | Why (in one line) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Wisdom Walk** (content/SEO/trust hub) | Everyone, eventually | **Google SEO (articles)** + **YouTube** | Highest-ROI organic channel; one hub feeds every other product and earns trust before the ask. |
| **Simply Pray** (PRIORITY, pre-launch app) | Devout Dana, Seeker Sam | **Instagram (Reels)** + **Facebook Groups / Reddit communities** for the waitlist | Reaches everyday believers visually; communities build a day-one waitlist before App Store launch. |
| **Consulting** (high-ticket B2B + your founder brand) | Pastor Paul | **LinkedIn (your personal account)** + **The Wisdom Walk SEO** | Decision-makers read LinkedIn; personal posts get 4x reach and book strategy calls. |
| **HopeStack** (counseling CRM, B2B, beta/waitlist) | Care Director Carol | **LinkedIn (personal + targeted)** + **SEO articles** | Tiny, specific buyer pool reached precisely; HIPAA-sensitive trust built through expertise, not hype. |
| **Heartward** (AI theology Q&A, low-price consumer) | Seeker Sam, Devout Dana | **YouTube/Shorts + TikTok** (ride the Wisdom Walk engine) | Question-driven discovery; lowest revenue per customer, so it must reuse content you already make. |

The unlock for your time budget: **don't build five marketing machines — build one (The Wisdom Walk) and point pieces of it at each product.** A single article on "How to actually pray when you have no words" can rank on Google, become a YouTube video, a Reels clip for Simply Pray, and a LinkedIn post about prayer tech for pastors. One effort, four placements.

Decisive priorities, given your constraints:
- **Do first, free:** Wisdom Walk SEO + your personal LinkedIn. These cost time, not money, and serve consulting (your revenue today) and Simply Pray (your priority).
- **Do next:** Instagram + one community for Simply Pray's waitlist (start 6-8 weeks before App Store launch — pre-launch marketing lifts day-one retention meaningfully, [MobileAction, 2025](https://www.mobileaction.co/blog/pre-launch-marketing/)).
- **Don't yet:** Heartward gets no dedicated effort — it piggybacks on Wisdom Walk content. Don't open TikTok *and* Instagram *and* Pinterest at once.
- **Where the ~$1,000 eventually goes:** save most of it. Light spend on email tooling and an SEO keyword tool now; hold paid ads until an organic message is proven to convert.

## Examples

- **Wisdom Walk → consulting call:** You publish "5 questions to ask before deploying private AI in your church." Pastor Paul finds it on Google, trusts your depth, clicks "free 30-min strategy call." Same article reposted from your personal LinkedIn (not the company page) gets 4x the reach.
- **Simply Pray waitlist, pre-launch:** You join 3-4 active Facebook prayer groups and a subreddit like r/Christianity. You don't pitch; you answer "how do I build a prayer habit?" honestly, mention the ACTS method, and link a waitlist page. Eight weeks of this = a real launch-day audience instead of crickets.
- **HopeStack to Carol:** You post on LinkedIn: "Counseling centers: here's what HIPAA-aligned intake actually requires in 2026." Carol, a care director, reads it, sees you understand her compliance pain, and joins the beta waitlist. You'd never find her on TikTok.
- **Heartward for free:** Your Wisdom Walk video answering "Is doubt a sin?" gets cut into a 45-second Short. Seeker Sam watches, clicks to ask Heartward a follow-up question. Zero extra production.

## Common mistakes

- **Being everywhere.** Six half-built profiles beat by one strong one. With 2-5 hrs/week, two platforms is the ceiling.
- **Posting from the company page instead of *you*.** For consulting and HopeStack, your personal LinkedIn voice (pastor + engineer) outperforms a logo by ~4x.
- **Treating all five products as one audience.** Carol (B2B compliance) and Sam (seeking faith) require completely different platforms and tones.
- **Selling in faith communities.** Trust-driven niches punish pitching. Serve first; the link comes last, sparingly. Read each group's rules — many ban promotion.
- **Launching Simply Pray cold.** No waitlist = no day-one momentum. Start community presence 6-8 weeks early.
- **Compliance slip on HopeStack.** Never use real client stories or PHI (Protected Health Information — any identifiable patient data) in marketing. Keep it general and educational.
- **Chasing TikTok virality first.** Fun, but low-trust and time-hungry. Search (Google/YouTube) compounds; trends evaporate.
- **Quitting SEO at week 6.** It takes ~4-6 months to show results. That's normal, not failure.

## First 3 actions for Joshua

1. **This week (≈2 hrs):** Set up a free email/waitlist tool (MailerLite or Beehiiv) and put a Simply Pray waitlist signup on simplypray.io. This is your priority product — give it a place to capture interest now.
2. **This week (≈1 hr):** Optimize your *personal* LinkedIn profile (pastor + engineer building faith tech) and write one post linking a Wisdom Walk article. This serves consulting (today's revenue) and HopeStack at once.
3. **Next 2 weeks (≈2-3 hrs/wk):** Publish one Wisdom Walk article targeting a real question your people Google (use the free Google "People also ask" box for ideas), then repurpose it into one LinkedIn post and one Instagram Reel for Simply Pray. One piece, three placements — your whole system in miniature.
