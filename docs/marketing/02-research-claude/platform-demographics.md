# Platform demographics (who uses each platform in 2026)

> A plain map of which kinds of people hang out on each social platform, so you spend your limited time where your people actually are.

## Beginner explanation

"Platform demographics" is just a fancy way of saying: *who* is on each app. Demographics = the describable traits of a group of people — their age, gender, income, where they live, and what they care about. When marketers say "Instagram skews young" or "Facebook skews older," they're talking demographics.

Why care? Because every social app is a *different room full of different people*. Posting the same thing everywhere is like preaching identically at a youth lock-in, a hospital bedside, and a deacon's meeting. The words may be true everywhere, but the room changes what lands. Knowing the room is step one.

A few terms you'll see, defined plainly:

- **MAU (monthly active users):** how many real people open the app at least once a month. A "size of the crowd" number.
- **Skew:** which direction a group leans. "Skews male" means more men than women. "Skews 25-34" means that age band is the biggest slice.
- **Organic:** free reach — people find your post without you paying. (Your strategy is organic-first: prove the message works for free before spending a dime.)
- **B2B / B2C / B2B2C:** Business-to-Business (you sell to a church or org), Business-to-Consumer (you sell to one person), and Business-to-Business-through-Consumer (a church pays, but individual members use it — like Simply Pray's $49/mo Church plan).

Here's the 2026 lay of the land for U.S. adults, from Pew Research (Nov 2025): YouTube reaches 84%, Facebook 71%, Instagram 50%, TikTok 37%, Reddit 26% ([Pew, 2025](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/11/20/americans-social-media-use-2025/)). Globally the giants are even bigger — Facebook ~3.07 billion users, Instagram ~3 billion, YouTube ~2.58 billion, TikTok ~1.99 billion ([Sprout Social, Feb 2026](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/new-social-media-demographics/)).

## Why it matters

You have 2-5 hours a week and ~$1,000/month. You cannot be everywhere, and trying to is the single fastest way to burn out with nothing to show. Demographics let you *subtract* — to confidently ignore platforms where your people aren't, and go deep on the two or three where they are.

It also matters because your niche is **trust-driven and compliance-sensitive**. A counseling director (HopeStack) and a lead pastor (Simply Pray Church plan) are not impulse buyers. The platform shapes the trust you can build: a thoughtful LinkedIn post reads as credible; a frantic TikTok trend-chase can read as gimmicky to a 55-year-old senior pastor. Right message, wrong room, lost trust.

## How it applies to YOUR products

You have one founder brand and five offers, each aimed at a different person. Match the persona to the room:

- **The Wisdom Walk (your content/SEO hub):** This isn't really a "platform demographic" play — it's the *home base* everything links back to. YouTube (84% reach, all ages) and search are its natural amplifiers. Treat YouTube less as "video social" and more as the world's second-largest search engine where a "What is the ACTS prayer method?" video lives for years.
- **Simply Pray (PRIORITY, pre-launch iOS app):** Your individual user is **Devout Dana**. Dana lives on Instagram and increasingly Facebook for the 30-49 faith crowd. Instagram is 80% reach among 18-29-year-olds ([Pew, 2025](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/11/20/americans-social-media-use-2025/)) — good for younger believers — while the **Church plan ($49/mo, B2B2C)** is sold to Pastor Paul, who is reachable on Facebook (most popular with the 30-49 group, ~80%) and LinkedIn. Build a *waitlist* now; the platform's job pre-launch is email capture, not sales.
- **HopeStack (counseling CRM, HIPAA-aligned, highest revenue-per-customer):** Your buyer is **Care Director Carol** — a professional. This is a **LinkedIn** product. LinkedIn skews 25-34 (~33%) but is where the 30-49 professional decision-makers and high-income users concentrate ([Sprout Social, Feb 2026](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/new-social-media-demographics/)). Do not chase Carol on TikTok.
- **Heartward (AI theological Q&A, $5-$25/mo, low-ARPU consumer):** Your people are **Devout Dana** and **Seeker Sam**. Seekers ask questions where questions live: Reddit (26% of U.S. adults, heavily 19-29, fairly gender-balanced) and YouTube search. Reddit is a *listen-and-help* room, not a billboard.
- **Solomon Solutions consulting (high-ticket B2B, free 30-min call):** Pastor Paul and ministry leaders. **LinkedIn + Facebook + your YouTube/Wisdom Walk credibility.** The founder brand ("built by a pastor, engineered for the church") is the asset here.

Given organic-first, no audience, and 2-5 hrs/week: pick **two** platforms to start. The strongest pairing for your ecosystem is **YouTube/long-content (The Wisdom Walk) + LinkedIn (consulting + HopeStack)**, with Instagram or Facebook as the third once the first two have traction.

## Examples

- **Pastor Paul on Facebook:** You write one LinkedIn post — "3 systems every 200-person church outgrows" — repurpose it as a Facebook post in pastor groups. Facebook's 30-49 lead-pastor crowd sees it; one books your free strategy call. One idea, two rooms, zero ad spend.
- **Seeker Sam on Reddit:** Someone in r/Christianity asks, "How do I even start praying?" You answer warmly, mention the ACTS method, and link a Wisdom Walk article (not a hard pitch). Sam reads it, later finds Heartward. That's trust earned, not bought.
- **Devout Dana on Instagram:** A 30-second Reel walking through one line of the ACTS method (Adoration → "tell God one thing you admire about Him today") drives Simply Pray waitlist signups from believers 18-49.
- **Care Director Carol on LinkedIn:** A post on "the hidden cost of spreadsheet-based intake at a counseling center" speaks directly to Carol's daily pain — and HIPAA-aligned HopeStack is the quiet answer.

## Common mistakes

- **Being on five platforms with 2-5 hours/week.** You'll post mediocre content everywhere and great content nowhere. Pick two.
- **Chasing TikTok because "that's where youth are."** True for reach, but it's a high-volume, trend-driven room that eats time. Your highest-value buyers (Paul, Carol) aren't making decisions there. (Fact: TikTok skews 25-34; opinion: it's the wrong *first* room for you.)
- **Treating LinkedIn like a billboard.** It rewards thoughtful, personal, leadership writing — exactly your strength as a pastor-engineer. Don't post brochures.
- **Selling before you serve — especially in faith spaces.** Reddit and church Facebook groups punish pitch-first behavior. Help first; the offer comes later.
- **Confusing reach with fit.** Facebook reaches 71% of U.S. adults, but reaching everyone isn't the goal — reaching *Pastor Paul* is.
- **Compliance slips.** Never post anything that implies real client/counselee details for HopeStack, and never imply Simply Pray is "live" on the App Store before it is. In a trust niche, one careless post costs more than it earns.
