# Organic social media strategy

> Earning attention on social platforms by posting helpful, free content people actually want, instead of paying for ads.

## Beginner explanation

"Organic" social media means the posts you publish for free on a platform like Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook, or LinkedIn. You make a video or graphic, hit publish, and the platform shows it to some people at no cost to you. "Paid" social media is the opposite: you give the platform money (an "ad spend") and it pushes your content in front of a chosen audience. The first is unpaid; the second is rented attention ([Hootsuite](https://blog.hootsuite.com/organic-vs-paid-social-media/)).

A few terms you'll see constantly:

- **Reach** = how many different people saw your post. **Impressions** = total times it was shown (one person seeing it twice = 1 reach, 2 impressions).
- **Engagement** = actions people take: likes, comments, shares, saves, follows. **Engagement rate** = engagement divided by reach (or followers), as a percentage. It's the clearest signal that content landed.
- **The algorithm** = the platform's behind-the-scenes ranking system that decides whose posts get shown. It rewards content that earns engagement quickly, especially shares and saves.
- **CTA (call to action)** = the one next step you ask the viewer to take ("Join the waitlist," "Book a free call," "Read the full article").
- **Cadence** = how often you post on a regular schedule.

Here's the honest part for a beginner: on most platforms, the algorithm only shows your post to a small slice of your own followers unless it performs well. In 2025 the average organic reach was roughly **1.65% of followers on Facebook and about 3.5% on Instagram** ([Sprout Social via Zen Agency](https://zen.agency/the-truth-about-organic-reach-in-2025-is-paid-advertising-the-only-way/)). So organic is not "free reach to everyone." It's a slow, compounding way to build trust and an audience. The payoff comes from content quality and consistency over months, not from one viral post.

The standard playbook is **content first, then CTA**. The most-cited version is the **80/20 rule**: about 80% of your posts give value (teach, inspire, encourage, tell a story) and roughly 20% promote your product. When you've earned trust with the 80%, the 20% feels welcome instead of pushy ([FSE Digital](https://www.fsedigital.com/blog/social-media-strategy-for-2025-why-the-80-20-rule-still-wins/)).

## Why it matters

Organic is the right starting move for someone with no audience, no list, and a small budget. It costs time, not ad dollars, and it builds the one thing your faith niche runs on: trust. Paid ads can buy clicks, but they can't buy credibility, and they stop the moment you stop paying.

Two facts make organic worth your limited hours. First, **short-form video (clips under 60 seconds) is the highest-leverage format right now.** Two out of three consumers say short-form video is the most engaging content type, it gets about 2.5x more engagement than long-form, and **82% of marketers say social video gives them positive ROI** ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-video-statistics/)). Its presence in search results has also grown 183% in two years, so video now drives discovery, not just scrolling ([HubSpot](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/short-form-video-trends)).

Second, organic doubles as cheap market research. Before you spend a dollar on ads, your free posts tell you which message, which product, and which audience actually resonates. That is exactly the "prove the message free, then add paid" sequence your budget calls for.

## How it applies to YOUR products

You have five things to talk about but only 2-5 hours a week, so do not run five accounts. Concentrate on **one founder-led brand voice** ("built by a pastor, engineered for the church") and let that single voice point to whichever product fits each post.

- **The Wisdom Walk is your engine.** Treat your articles as the source material. Each article becomes one short video, one carousel, and a few text posts. This respects your time (write once, slice many ways) and feeds SEO and trust for everything else.
- **Simply Pray is the priority, and it's pre-launch, so your organic CTA is "join the waitlist," not "download."** Post short videos teaching the ACTS prayer method (Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication). This is pure 80% value content for Devout Dana, and the waitlist is the soft 20% CTA. You're building a launch-day audience for free.
- **Consulting (Solomon Solutions)** is your high-ticket B2B play and fits LinkedIn best. Post short, plain-English takes on church systems, leadership, and private AI for ministry, aimed at Pastor Paul. The CTA is your free 30-minute strategy call. You only need a handful of the right viewers, not a huge audience.
- **HopeStack** (counseling-center CRM, HIPAA-aligned) is beta/waitlist and serves Care Director Carol, a small, specific B2B audience. Don't chase volume. Post occasional credibility content (care-team workflow tips, why privacy matters) on LinkedIn, CTA to the waitlist.
- **Heartward** (AI theological Q&A) is low-priced consumer and pairs naturally with Devout Dana and Seeker Sam. Answer real faith questions in short videos; that *is* the product demo. Keep it light in the mix so it doesn't crowd out Simply Pray.

Given $1,000/month and 2-5 hours/week, stay organic-first. Spend money on tools and maybe a small Simply Pray waitlist ad test later, after free posts prove which message converts.

## Examples

- **ACTS teaching clip (Simply Pray, Dana):** 40-second video, you on camera: "Most people freeze when they try to pray. Here's a 4-word framework that fixes it." Walk through A-C-T-S. CTA: "Simply Pray builds this into a daily habit, link to join the waitlist."
- **Pastor-to-pastor LinkedIn post (Consulting, Paul):** "Your church has 6 software tools that don't talk to each other. Here are the 3 questions I ask before integrating any of them." End with: "If you want a second set of eyes, I do free 30-minute strategy calls."
- **Repurposed Wisdom Walk article (whole ecosystem):** An article on "praying through anxiety" becomes a 30-second video (Simply Pray), a 5-slide carousel of the key points (saveable), and a quote graphic. One hour of writing, a week of posts.
- **Seeker Sam answer (Heartward):** Video titled "Is it okay to doubt and still have faith?" You answer warmly in 45 seconds. CTA: "Heartward lets you ask questions like this anytime."
- **Behind-the-scenes trust builder:** A quick clip of you, a pastor who codes, explaining *why* you built these tools. Faith audiences reward authentic, unscripted moments over polished ads ([Discipls](https://www.churchsocial.ai/blog/nonprofit-social-media-best-practices)).

## Common mistakes

- **Selling before serving.** Posting nothing but "download my app" with no value content. Trust-driven faith audiences disengage fast. Honor the 80/20 mix.
- **Spreading too thin.** Five products on five platforms with five hours. Pick one or two platforms (start with one short-video platform plus LinkedIn for B2B) and one weekly content engine (Wisdom Walk).
- **Chasing follower count instead of the right people.** HopeStack only needs a few counseling directors; consulting needs a few pastors. Vanity metrics mislead. Watch saves, shares, comments, and waitlist signups.
- **Inconsistency.** Posting 10 times one week, then vanishing for a month. The algorithm and your audience both reward steady cadence. Three good posts a week beats a sporadic flood.
- **Promising what isn't live.** Simply Pray and HopeStack aren't shipping yet, so always CTA to a *waitlist*, never "buy/download now." Misleading CTAs erode the exact trust you're building.
- **Faith-niche compliance slips.** Don't imply HopeStack is a medical/clinical authority or share anything resembling real client data; keep theological claims humble and on-brand. In a trust-and-compliance-sensitive niche, tone matters as much as accuracy.

## First 3 actions for Joshua

1. **Pick one platform plus LinkedIn, and set up the founder account (about 1 hour).** Choose Instagram *or* TikTok for short video (your Dana/Sam audience), keep LinkedIn for Paul/Carol. Use one consistent bio: "Pastor + engineer building faith tech for the church," linking to The Wisdom Walk.
2. **Build a 2-week content batch from one Wisdom Walk article (about 2 hours).** Have AI draft 6 short scripts and captions from the article; you approve and lightly edit. Film 3-4 talking-head clips on your phone in one sitting. Schedule them in the free Metricool or Buffer plan at 3 posts/week ([Slate](https://slateteams.com/blog/how-often-to-post-to-social-media)).
3. **Set your 80/20 cadence and one CTA per product (about 30 min/week ongoing).** Of every 5 posts, make 4 pure value and 1 promotional. Rotate CTAs: Simply Pray waitlist, free strategy call, read on The Wisdom Walk. Each Friday, check saves/shares/comments and note which topic won, that's your free message-testing data before any paid spend.
